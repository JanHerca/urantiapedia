# README

## Papers with potential problems when creating the readable version

- Several points in a line `. . .`: Papers 15 and 35
- Decimal degress `&deg;`: Papers 24 (24:1.11) and 124 (124:1.9)
- One quarter fraction `&frac14;`: Paper 57 (57:0.1)
- Spanish billions are no readable in IBM Watson `\d+\.\d+\.\d+\.\d+\.\d+` or `\d+,\d+,\d+,\d+,\d+`: Paper 15, 23 (23:3.3) and 24 (24:7.5) (https://clevert.com.br/t/en/numbers-to-words/index)
- Spanish abbreviations for AD and BC: 
  - `a. de J. C.`: Papers 92 (92:4.6), 93 (93:1.1), 95 (95:1.1), 96 (96:1.11), 135 (135:0.1) (135:0.3)
  - `a. de J.C.`: Papers 74, 77, 78, 79, 80, 81, 94, 121, 122, 123, 124, 143
  - `d. de J.C.`: Papers 31, 62, 79, 119, 121, 123, 124, 126, 127, 128, 129, 134
- Roman numbers: `I`, `V`, `VI`, `X`, etc., `(I[XV]|V|X|V?I{1,3})\. `: Paper 0, 9 and 30. Also paper 95:4 can contain roman numbers when talking about Bible chapters.
- Character `$` is used in paper 42 to format some words. Must be removed.
- Paper 41:4 has a math number.


## In other languages

- French: `a. de J. C.` is `av. J.-C` = `avant Jésus-Christ`; `d. de J.C.` is `apr. J.-C` = `après Jésus-Christ`
- Portuguese: `a.C.` or `a.C` = `antes de Cristo`; `d.C.` or `d.C` = `depois de Cristo`
- Italian: `a.C.` = `avanti Cristo`; `d.C.` = `dopo Cristo`
- Deutsch: `v. Chr.` = `vor Christus`; `n. Chr.` = `nach Christus`
