Lynching and dueling represent the unwillingness of the individual to surrender private redress to the state. [1]

Early wars were fought between tribes as a whole, but in later times, when two individuals in different tribes had a dispute, instead of both tribes fighting, the two disputants engaged in a duel. [2] Dueling is a modern survival of the trial by ordeal. [3]
