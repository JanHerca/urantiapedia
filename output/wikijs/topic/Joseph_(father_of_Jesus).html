At Jesus’ first Passover. [1]

Attitude

Angry with Jesus about Scythopolis games. [2] Explained reasons for family discipline. [3] Faithfully discharged parental responsibilities. [4] Held to Babylonian view of Judaism. [5] Held spiritual concept of Messiah. [6] Mild-mannered; conscientious; subject to mild spiritual discouragement. [7] Never failed fully to discuss Jesus’ questions. [8] Perplexed over Gabriel’s message; dream. [9]

Background and heritage

8 brothers and sisters. [10] Average person of day and generation. [11] Black-eyed brunet. [12] Blue race, Nodites, Sumerians, Abraham were ancestors; genealogies. [13] Educated far above average. [14] Fluent speaker of Aramaic and Greek. [15] Marriage to Mary in March, 8 B.C. [16] Poor at birth of Jesus. [17]

Conferred with Zacharias in Jerusalem. [18] Did not know cause of earthquake. [19] Family became believers in Jesus. [20] Found Jesus teaching in temple. [21] Gabriel chose family for incarnation. [22] Killed September 25, 8 A.D. working on Herod’s building in Sepphoris. [23] Ordered Jesus not to draw. [24] Owned a donkey. [25] Owned houses with partner Jacob the stone mason. [26] Owned property in Nazareth and Capernaum. [27] Prevailed upon to remain in Bethlehem for a year. [28] Removed parchment from doorpost. [29] Sabbath walks with Jesus. [30] Supply shop taken over by his brother. [31] Supported his parents; father recently disabled. [32] Trouble with Jesus regarding his prayers. [33] Unfortunate he could not have lived. [34]

Work

Became builder; trebled family income. [35] Employed as foreman in Alexandria. [36] In Sepphoris. [37]
