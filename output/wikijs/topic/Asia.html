Geology

The backbone of the Asiatic land mass reached a height of almost nine miles at the time of the maximum land emergence. [1] A wealth of data on geological processes in Asia is offered in The Urantia Book. [2] The eastern coast line of Asia hovers over the precipice of its adjoining oceanic depths, threatening to slide into a watery grave. [3]

Races in Asia

Asia is the homeland of the human race. [4] Andonites were scattered all over central Asia in 15,000 B.C. [5] Most of the animals best suited to domestication were found in Asia, especially in the central to southwest regions. [6] Yellow race drove red race from Asia. [7]

No red man ever returned to Asia. But throughout Siberia, China, central Asia, India, and Europe they left behind much of their stock blended with the other colored races. [8] Yellow race also drove Andonites from Asia. [9] The last of cannibalism in Asia was this eating of executed criminals. [10]

The early teachers of the Salem religion penetrated to the remotest tribes of Africa and Eurasia, ever preaching Machiventa’s gospel of man’s faith and trust in the one universal God as the only price of obtaining divine favor. [11]
