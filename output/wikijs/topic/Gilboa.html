Philistines defeated Saul at Mount Gilboa. King Saul took his life there. [1] Mary and Joseph camped on the slopes of Mount Gilboa on their way to Bethlehem to register. [2] This mount was visible from the hill near the house of Jesus in Nazareth. [3] Jesus passed by with his parents when they first took him to Jerusalem. [4] Between the months of September and October 27 Jesus and the apostles established their camp on Mount Gilboa. [5] On October 27 the apostles of Jesus held a conference with the apostles of John on Mount Gilboa. [6] Matthew and Philip were expelled from a Samaritan village on the slopes of Mount Gilboa on their way to the Feast of Tabernacles in the year 29. [7]
