The Son of Man confidently declared to Satan, “Get you behind me,” settling the status of agelong enemies. [1] A kingdom divided against itself cannot stand; if Satan casts out Satan, he is divided against himself, and his kingdom cannot stand. [2] Satan issued the Lucifer Manifesto on the sea of glass, proclaiming allegiance only to Lucifer, the “God of liberty”. [3]

Jesus refuted the accusation that he performed miracles by the power of Satan, stating that a kingdom divided against itself cannot stand and that one who is greater than devils stands in their midst. [4]

Judas's betrayal had Satan's full approval, as Jesus knew his hour had come and he faced his enemies with the safety and salvation of his chosen followers in mind. [5] Satan, Lucifer's first lieutenant, was sent to advocate his cause on Urantia, but ultimately failed in his mission to thwart Michael's bestowal. [6]

Jesus faced the great temptation on Mount Hermon, revealing Satan and Caligastia, representing Lucifer, in a trial of human loyalty to the sovereignty of the universe. [7]

Satan, a primary Lanonandek of great brilliance, was assigned by Lucifer to advocate his cause on Urantia, but ultimately failed in their mission to thwart Michael's bestowal. [8]

Satan, a brilliant Lanonandek Son of light, visited Caligastia prior to rebellion, informing him of Lucifer's proposed “Declaration of Liberty” which led to the Prince's betrayal of trust. [9]

Status after rebellion

The Ancients of Days allowed Satan to visit apostate princes, as Michael petitioned to intern all personalities involved in the Lucifer rebellion. [10] Satan fell as lightning from heaven, symbolizing the cleansing of rebellion and sin in Norlatiadek after the triumph of Christ. [11]

Until Michael's bestowal mission was completed on Urantia, Satan freely roamed Satania, deceiving and misleading minds, but ultimately falling from heaven. [12] Satan is now detained on Jerusem prison worlds, ending his ability to visit any of the fallen worlds of Satania. [13] Satan sought to attend the Edentia conclave, but was unanimously rejected and fell from heaven like lightning. [14] Sympathy for Satan perished throughout Satania after the failed attempt to corrupt Michael on Urantia. [15]
