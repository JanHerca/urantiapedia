Peace treaties were sealed through blood drinking and offerings of maidens, a tradition dating back to the earliest peace missions. [1]

The first formal tribal treaty concerned intertribalizing a salt deposit, reflecting the historical significance of natural resources in early trade relations. [2]

World peace cannot be maintained by treaties or any other type of makeshift juggling with the sovereignties of nationalism; world law enforced by world government is necessary. [3]
