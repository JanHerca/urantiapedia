The Carrara marble, originating from modified limestone, can be found in Europe, particularly Germany and Russia, as well as in Africa and Australia, but is absent in the southern regions of South America due to a continuous marine deposit. [1] Limestone changed into marble through pressure and folding 300,000,000 years ago, altering the original character of the deposits. [2]
