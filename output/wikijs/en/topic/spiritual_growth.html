Definition

Morality originates in self-conscious reason, evolving beyond the animal realm, with the soul representing the potential survival value of human experience through moral choice, spiritual attainment, and the urge to know and be like God. [1]

Spiritual growth involves awakening to needs, discerning meanings, and discovering values, ultimately leading to a personality motivated by love, ministry, and worship of divinity's perfection ideals, forming the reality of religion over theological beliefs. [2]

The fruit of divinity is love, the greatest thing in the universe, revealed through truth, beauty, goodness, intellectual peace, social progress, moral satisfaction, spiritual joy, and cosmic wisdom. [3] Ascendington is where human self enters a new relationship with divine self, as the pilgrimage towards Paradise progresses. [4] Spirituality is both a sign of closeness to God and a gauge of how helpful one is to others. [5]

[6] Spiritual growth is the measure of Adjuster attunement and Fatherlikeness, leading to the discernment of transformative spiritual influences. [7] The never-ending voyage of discovery into the absoluteness of Father is the final quest of eternity for all universe personalities. [8] Every aspiring creature destined for perfection attainment partners with God in this magnificent adventure. [9]

The reality of spiritual growth transcends all human achievements and is unassailable, incontrovertible, superhuman, divine, indomitable, unquestioning, supreme, and eternal. [10] Spiritual growth involves creative activities in the superconscious realms of mortal mind. [11] Unvaryingly unconscious religious growth is a positive proof of superconscious creative activities in the economy of spiritual development. [12]

Achievement

Spiritual growth is measured by proximity to God and contribution to others, increasing one's ability to appreciate beauty, discern truth, and embody goodness through the removal of selfish love. [13] Spiritual growth progresses by faith and revelation, not by sight or material assurance. Spiritual growth unfolds steadily and deliberately by gradual stages, avoiding both stagnation and overrapid advancement, following a patient path akin to a drop of water descending in a succession of short falls. [17] Spiritual growth is achieved by the integration of truth, beauty, and goodness in the evolving creature's life experience. [18] Maintaining a living connection with Jesus is essential for bearing abundant spiritual fruit. [19] Spiritual growth is enhanced by obeying divine leadings, which leads to sanctification and spiritualization of the inner life. [20]

Spiritual growth is achieved through sincere and steadfast God-seeking decisions that lead to the evolution of selfhood beyond the material body to the immortal nature of the morontia soul and eventually to spirit reality. [22] Spiritual growth is achieved through genuine interest and love for higher ideals, not simply replacing desires through human willpower.". [23]

Inner spiritual progress is achieved through wholehearted devotion to supreme values, unaffected by temporal conditions or innate drives towards growth and self-realization. [24] Spiritual growth is dependent on knowledge and wisdom, but not survival. [25]

Spiritual growth relies on maintaining a living spiritual connection and consistently producing spiritual fruit by sharing one's spiritual blessings with others. [26]

The essence of spiritual growth lies in discovering the good and right technique of reacting to ever-recurring situations, guided by moral consciousness towards ethical and morontial values. [27]

Spiritual growth requires understanding divine rest, essential for effectively utilizing spirit energy, and seeking counsel on energy intake and balance from energy manipulators such as seraphim, cherubim, and sanobim. [28]

Spiritual growth is entirely dependent on the mind endowment of material creatures, which are influenced by the seven adjutant mind-spirits superimposed by the Infinite Spirit. [36] Spiritual growth thrives in the absence of external pressures. [37] Spiritual growth is the result of inherent motion and the divine capacity for action. [38]

Spiritual growth is the inevitable result of choosing Father's will, fostering the evolution of the immortal soul through experiential transformations of spiritual attainment. [39]

[40] The essence of spiritual growth lies in progressing from loyalty to love to filial relationships before attaining true freedom and self-realization. [41]
