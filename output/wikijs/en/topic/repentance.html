Adam and Eve's acceptable repentance led to them receiving Adjusters and becoming eligible for resurrection and rehabilitation. [1] Cain repented and received an Adjuster, leading to a new sense of spiritual guidance and superiority in Mesopotamia. [2] The Father seeks sinners before they think of repentance, finding joy in saving the lost. [3] God never stands in need of repentance for his all-wise and all-powerful actions, guiding creation with divine foreknowledge and unlimited insight. [4] The goodness of God leads erring man to repentance. [5] Confession, repentance, and prayer have led nations to mighty efforts of reform and courageous deeds of valorous achievement. [6] Repent sincerely, fear not the judgment of man, for true forgiveness awaits in the heavenly courts. [7]

Jesus taught the forgiveness of sin through faith in God, without penance or sacrifice, emphasizing that faith alone is the key to entering the Father's kingdom. [8] Jesus taught his apostles that faith, not good works, was the key to entering the Father's kingdom. [9] Judas said he repented after witnessing the crucifixion of Jesus and returning his payment to the Sanhedrin. [10] Understanding of intimate and loving relations prevents estrangements, which require repentance for readjustment. [11]

John the Baptist's message of “Repent and be baptized” evolved into a comforting call to flee from wrath and embrace the joy of the new kingdom, as Jesus preached. [12] Repentance was left optional for the apostles of Jesus, who preached “Believe and be baptized.”. [13] Preach repentance to those in need, but welcome those already seeking fellowship with God into the joyous kingdom. [14] Publicans and harlots repent before religious rulers, while the common people hear Jesus's words gladly. [15]

Recognition of the evil of one’s ways leads to repentance, forgiveness, and the transformation of calamity into blessing, as seeking the Great Supreme daily ensures finding forgiveness and mercy. [16] Restraint born of repentance leads to true happiness and peace of mind. [17] The repentant thief on the cross found salvation through his faith in Jesus. [18]
