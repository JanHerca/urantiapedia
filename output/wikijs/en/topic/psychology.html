Psychology studies religious reactions to society, but cannot delve into the true inner motives of religion; only theology, rooted in faith and revelation, can provide an intelligent account of religious experience. [1]

Theology studies the human spirit, and when combined with philosophy, it can never become a science, always relying on psychology for personal expression and understanding others' religions. [2]
