1. Archeozoic, prelife era.
    • 1 billion to 550 million years ago. [1]
  2. Proterozoic, life-dawn era.
    • 550 to 400 million years ago. [2]
  3. Paleozoic, marine-life era.
    • 400 to 150 million years ago. [3]
    • Cambrian, trilobite age.
      • 400 to 350 million years ago. [4]
    • Ordovician, invertebrate-animal age.
      • 350 to 300 million years ago. [5]
    • Silurian, coral and brachiopod age.
      • 300 to 275 million years ago. [6]
    • Devonian, age of plant life and fishes.
      • 270 to 220 million years ago. [7]
    • Carboniferous, age of fern forests and frogs.
      • 220 to 180 million years ago. [8]
    • Permian, seed-plant age and biologic tribulation.
      • 170 to 150 million years ago. [9]
  4. Mesozoic, early land-life era.
    • 150 to 50 million years ago. [10]
    • Triassic, early-reptilian age.
      • 150 to 125 million years ago. [11]
    • Jurassic, later-reptilian age.
      • 125 to 100 million years ago. [12]
    • Cretaceous, age of flowering plants and birds.
      • 100 to 50 million years ago. [13]
  5. Cenozoic, mammalian era.
    • 50 million years ago to the present. [14]
    • Eocene, early-mammal age.
      • 50 to 35 million years ago. [15]
    • Oligocene, advanced mammal age.
      • 35 to 25 million years ago. [16]
    • Miocene, age of elephant and horse.
      • 25 to 10 million years ago. [17]
    • Pliocene, mammalian migration stage.
      • 10 to 1 million years ago. [18]
    • Pleistocene, ice age.
      • 2 million to 100,000 years ago. [19]
    • Holocene, post-glacial period.
      • 35,000 years ago to the present. [20]
