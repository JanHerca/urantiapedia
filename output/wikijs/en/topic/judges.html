Jesus’ address to judges on behalf of the poor man emphasized the importance of impartiality, fairness, and integrity in the pursuit of justice. [1] Jesus wisely defended Jude before the military magistrate, warning him of potential trouble. [2]

Micah denounced rulers who judge for reward, priests who teach for hire, and prophets who divine for money, envisioning a future of freedom from superstition and priestcraft. [3]
