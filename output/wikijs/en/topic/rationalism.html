Rationalism leans toward the world of matter, while idealism inclines toward the spiritual, and leaning on metaphysics leads to skepticism and confusion in philosophy's interpretations of reality. [1]

Rationalism is wrong to assume that primitive beliefs precede the pursuit of values in religion, as religious values are the primary focus before forming interpretative beliefs. [2]
