James of Emmaus, along with his older brother Cleopas, were two shepherds, somewhat believers in Jesus, who were the fortunate witnesses of the seventh appearance of Jesus, while he was on the road to Emmaus. Curiously, the name of James does not appear in the gospels, although that of Cleopas does. [1]
