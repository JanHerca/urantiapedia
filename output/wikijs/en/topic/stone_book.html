The well-preserved stone pages hold the life records of Urantia, laid down in volume upon volume by the ancient inland seas. [1]

The oldest fossil-bearing stratum in the crust of the earth, containing remnants of early marine life, tells the story of life's gradual spread and evolution throughout the world, as recorded in the vast “stone book” of geologic history. [2]
