Among the earliest medicines were human secretions, roots, and various salts used by shamans to drive out disease spirits. [1] Marine animals evolved the ability to regulate salt levels in their bodies as oceans became saltier. [2] The apostles were sent forth as the precious salt of the earth, tasked with preserving and enhancing the world with their saving savor. [3] Early wars were fought over natural deposits like salt. [4]

The first group specialists were rock salt exporters and potters, with women making plain pottery and men creating the fancy pieces, while some tribes had women involved in sewing and weaving, and others had men. [5]

The finaliters, accumulating tried and true souls of time and space, maintain their own headquarters on Paradise and are forever proof against evil and secure against sin. [6] The first literature of man was a trade-promotion document, a salt advertisement. [7] You are the salt of the earth, with a saving savor; do not lose your value by seeking worldly honor and glory. [8] Primitives dipped food in ashes to obtain salt for savoring and self-gratification. [9] The council on science and art, led by Nod, greatly expanded trade with the improved salt. [10] Rock salt settled in great lagoons, cut off and evaporated over time, forming thick deposits in various regions around the world. [11] Rock salt deposits from the Silurian period are a key source of gas, oil, zinc, and lead, formed from ancient marine sediments and lagoons. [12] Life on Urantia began in a fresh-water ocean, but evolved to require a salty solution for the protoplasm to function. [13] The modern word “salary” is derived from the precious salt used as money in Jesus' time, enhancing flavor and preserving food. [14]
