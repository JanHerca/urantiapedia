El Shaddai represented divine Providence; the service rewarded with prosperity. [1] It represented the Egyptian concept of God of heaven. [2] Moses’ father-in-law was a Kenite worshiper of El Elyon, but the emancipator’s parents were believers in El Shaddai. [3]
