Amidists believe in the teaching of ultimate existence by Amida Buddha, seeking salvation through faith in his divine mercies. [1]

Religion is the enlistment of the totality of selfhood in loyal service to loving God and serving man, not a technique for attaining static and blissful peace of mind. [2] Nirvana is a state of supreme enlightenment and bliss, representing release from the material world. [3]
