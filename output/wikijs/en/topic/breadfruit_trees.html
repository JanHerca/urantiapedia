Around 90 million years ago, breadfruit trees emerged alongside angiosperms during the early Cretaceous period. These trees, along with fig trees and palms, rapidly spread across Europe and the western plains of North America, becoming prominent in the evolving plant landscape. Their arrival marked a significant expansion of land flora, though no new land animals appeared during this time. [1]
