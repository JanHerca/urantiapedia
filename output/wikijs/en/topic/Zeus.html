Zeus, head of the Greek pantheon, was an imposed man-god on a Mediterranean world dominated by mother cults, hindering the Greeks' development towards true monotheism. [1] Zeus' transformation into Jupiter symbolized the extensive cultural importation from Greece to Rome, greatly influencing Roman religion. [2]
