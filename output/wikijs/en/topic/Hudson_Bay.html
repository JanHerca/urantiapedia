In northeastern Canada around Hudson Bay, extensive granite elevations contain stone from preoceanic ages, modified by intense metamorphic experiences. [1]

A chance meeting on the shores of Hudson Bay led to the merging of an Indian tribe and an Eskimo group, marking the only contact between North American red men and another human stock for thousands of years. [2]
