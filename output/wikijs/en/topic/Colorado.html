The Morrison beds of Colorado, Montana, and Wyoming contain abundant fresh-water fossils. [1] Pikes Peak and Longs Peak are examples of mountains that were re-elevated after erosion. [2] The Florissant fossil beds of Colorado belong to the later years of these far-distant times. [3]
