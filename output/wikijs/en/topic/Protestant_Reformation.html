The Protestant Reformation disrupted the unity of Christianity in Europe, leading to the synthesis of a new religion in the Orient, Sikhism. [1]

The fifteenth century saw a division in Christianity in Europe due to the inelasticity of the church, while in the Orient, a new religion, Sikhism, was synthesized from Islam, Hinduism, and Buddhism. [2]
