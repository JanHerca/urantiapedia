North America stood as one of the two extensive and fertile open hunting areas during the opening of the Andite era. [1]

The configuration of continents and oceans strongly influences peace or war, offering North America a unique opportunity for peaceful, continuous development. [2] The future of North America hinges on the quality of racial factors allowed to enter its population. [3]

Geology

About geology of North America see papers 59, 60, 61, in links bellow. [4] North America rose out of Pacific depths along with Europe, Africa, Australia, South America, and Antarctica, as the Pacific Ocean sank. [5] 500 million years ago, North and South America began their slow westward drift as marine life thrived on Urantia. [6]

North America experienced 24 great floods during the Cretaceous period, marking the end of extensive marine invasions and the beginning of a long history of land dominance. [7]

180,000,000 years ago marked the beginning of North America's modern mountains and the connection between eastern America and western Europe through Greenland. [8] North America is rich in marine-life fossils, ranging from the very first and oldest layers to the later strata of different periods. [9]

Humans

Enormous herds of horses and camels roamed the Miocene plains of North America in an age of evolving mammalian life. [10] The red race was driven from Asia to North America by their yellow brethren. [11]

The red race in North America remained isolated from the Andite offspring of Adam and Eve, spreading out as nomadic tribes practicing agriculture to a small extent. [12]

The discovery of North America by white races around A.D. 1000 marked the first contact between the indigenous peoples and Europeans, who had previously remained isolated from each other for millennia. [13]
