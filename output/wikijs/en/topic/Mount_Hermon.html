Mount Hermon, glistening with perpetual snow, stood majestically in the north as Jesus and Joseph took their Sabbath afternoon walks on the high hill near their home in Nazareth. [1] Mount Hermon, visible from Caesarea-Philippi, held a special significance for Jesus and his apostles during their final journey together. [2]

Jesus

The Adjuster led him to Mount Hermon to complete his full consecration to his lifework on earth. [3] On Mount Hermon, Jesus defeated Caligastia, becoming the Planetary Prince of Urantia. [4] Jesus endured temptation on Mount Hermon, ultimately achieving the sovereignty of his universe. [5] Jesus experienced the transfiguration on Mount Hermon, preparing for the events that would follow. [6] Jesus inaugurated his 4th phase of ministry on Mount Hermon. [7]
