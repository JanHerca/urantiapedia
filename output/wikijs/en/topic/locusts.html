The locusts appeared along with the rest of the insects, overspreading continents with giant dragonflies and four-inch cockroaches. [1]

John the Baptist ate locusts and honey, living simply along the valley with his adopted orphan, Ezda, while tending to his herds of sheep and goats in stone shelters. [2]
