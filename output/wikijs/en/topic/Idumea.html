During the times of Jesus, the Jews were unusually apprehensive and suspicious as they were ruled by the outsider Herod the Idumean, who cleverly ingratiated himself with Roman rulers. [1] Pontius Pilate was procurator of Judea, Samaria, and Idumea during the trial of Jesus before him in A.D. 30. [2]
