The apostles gathered and rubbed grain in their hands on a Sabbath day, which provoked the indignation of a group of spies of the Sanhedrin. [1] It was the custom of many Near Eastern peoples to throw grain upon the newlyweds; this was a magical rite which was supposed to insure fecundity. [2]
