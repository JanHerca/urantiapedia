Cease useless yearning and bravely do that which concerns the establishment of the kingdom. [1]

Defined as

Indolence is evident proof of moral immaturity in individuals striving for intellectual and emotional maturity within human society. [2]

Religion drives humanity from its natural state of intellectual inertia towards higher levels of reason and wisdom, fostering civilization and societal continuity. [3] The increasing dangers of indolence highlight service insensitivity. [4] Will you fail the Supreme personality of the universes by the slothful animalistic retrogression? [5]

Advices

“Deliver us from inertia and sustain us in our progress along the path of truth this day.”. [6]

The young man sought Jesus to escape his sorrows, but Jesus urged him to rise up, harness his physical and intellectual abilities, and conquer the evils of inaction with the power of living faith. [7] Few on Urantia cultivate courageous cosmic thinking, despite the innate cosmic responses found in the cosmic mind of all will creatures. [8]

The inertia of primitive man acts as a biologic safety brake against the dangers of a rapidly advancing civilization, ultimately preventing ruinous maladjustment. [9] The human intellect protests against weaning from nonspiritual energies, necessitating a struggle for growth and spiritual insight. [10]

Jesus' disciples should always be active, positive, equipped with spiritual weapons to conquer the world with forgiveness, good will, and love, overcoming evil with good, hate with love, fear with courageous faith in truth. [11] Man can reject survival by selfishness, slothfulness, and sinfulness. [12]

Men often seek to escape the rigors of truly religious activities through resorting to a retreat to the false shelter of stereotyped religious doctrines and dogmas. [13] Primitive communism put a premium on inactivity and idleness, stifling industry and ambition, ultimately giving way to a higher social order. [14] The profit motive keeps otherwise slothful individuals hard at work, but it does not need to remain selfish in its objectives. [15]

Do not be passive dreamers, supinely trusting in a fictitious Providence, but be valiant defenders of righteousness and aggressive preachers of the gospel. [16]
