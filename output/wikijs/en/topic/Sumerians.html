Around the time of Hammurabi's rule, the Sumerians were absorbed into the ranks of the northern Semites following severe reverses at the hands of the northern Suites and Guites. [1] Apostate Sethite descendants ruled individual Sumerian city-states, hindering the formation of powerful confederations due to deity jealousy. [2] Civilized man, following in the footsteps of the Sumerians, distinguished between inanimate and animate, bridging the gulfs between spirit and energy. [3] Dravidian commerce with Sumeria facilitated early city-building and extensive trade by land and sea. [4]

The rulership of the Mesopotamian city state of Sumer shifted among various valley cities, including Akkad, Kish, and Ur, before the Sumerians were absorbed into the northern Semites around 2500 B.C. [5] The peace-loving Sumerians were greatly respected as teachers, directors, and civil rulers by all peoples from Egypt to India. [6] By 5000 B.C., the purest Adamite strains were in Sumeria, spreading their biologic and cultural residue to the rest of the fertile crescent. [7] The Sumerians acknowledged the first and second Edens but viewed the garden dwellers as alien due to pride in their own Nodite culture. [8]

The sudden appearance of the Sumerians in Mesopotamia, with a fully developed culture and language unrelated to Semitic but bearing similarities to Aryan tongues, remains a mystery that baffles investigators. [9]

The Sumerians, descendants of the pre-Sumerian Nodites, lived for thousands of years at the mouth of the Tigris and Euphrates rivers, blending their Andite ancestry with Nodite culture to create a unique civilization that greatly influenced neighboring regions. [10] The Sumerians revived the art of pottery making during Adam's time, spreading the craft throughout the Eastern Hemisphere. [11]

Despite being overrun by barbarian cavalrymen, the united Sumerians managed to defend themselves due to superior intelligence, better weapons, and a system of military canals, preventing any city groups from overcoming them. [12] The Sumerians, of Nodite and Adamite origin, emerged suddenly with a full-grown and superior culture in Mesopotamia. [13] The Sumerians emerged suddenly with a full-grown, superior culture in Mesopotamia, originating 200,000 years ago after the submergence of Dalamatia. [14] The Sumerians used castor oil and opium as medicine, a practice inherited by the Greeks and Egyptians. [15]
