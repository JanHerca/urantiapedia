Ganid was a brilliant Indian boy who was taught by Jesus during a journey with him and his father Gonod. [1] The first meeting with Jesus took place during Passover of the year 22, in Jerusalem. [2] Ganid suffered a serious illness in Cyprus and was lovingly cared for by Jesus and his father. [3]

He put into practice the teachings of his teacher when in Antioch he spoke with an employee of his father who was dissatisfied with the tasks assigned to him. [4] Together with Jesus, he produced a translated synopsis of the best texts on the religions of the world that they found in the library of Alexandria. [5] He was a worthy successor to his eminent father. [6]
