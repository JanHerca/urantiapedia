It is a typical public place of the Roman Empire where shows and games were offered. Some amphitheaters mentioned in The Urantia Book are:

  • At Lake Urmia, a building for lectures dedicated to the “spirit of religion”, where Jesus spent some time. [1]
  • In Caesarea, with capacity for 20,000 sat spectators. Here Jesus attended a performance. [2]
  • In the Decapolis, in Scythopolis, where Jesus and his father attended the annual athletic games. [3]
  • After attending the Scythopolis anphiteater, young Jesus suggested his father Joseph building a similar amphitheater in Nazareth, causing the anger of his father. [4]

In architectural spheres there are constructions of the same type:

  • On Edentia, the amphiteater encircling the sea of glass, the receiving area of Edentia. [5]
  • On Jerusem, the amphiteater encircling the broadcast-receiving station, with seats for five billion. [6]
