Renunciation was the negative form of propitiation, with sacrifice becoming the positive form, while gifts and bribes given to men were transformed into dedicated sacrifices for the gods in ancient practices of divine worship. [1]

Primitive concepts of renunciation led to the development of fasting as a common practice in religious evolution, ultimately influencing many modern theological systems. [2]
