Only a poet can discern poetry in the commonplace prose of routine existence. [1] Poetry is mortal morontia, serving as man's attempt to transcend material constraints and embrace spiritual truths. [2] Without incantations, poetry would not have originated in religion, handicapping the cultural development of society. [3]

Rhythmic poetry can relieve profound philosophy with restfulness, balancing the strain of living and feelings of insecurity through faith contemplation and contemplation of the Father. [4]
