Anaxando is a young Greek with whom Jesus spoke in Caesarea when he was traveling in the Mediterranean. [1] He became quartermaster of the Roman centurion named Cornelius, who was made a believer by Peter. [2] Converted to Christianity by the Apostle Philip. [3] He accidentally perished while helping wounded in the massacre of 20,000 Jews, which occurred in Caesarea in 66 AD. [4]
