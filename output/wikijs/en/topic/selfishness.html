Material feelings and human emotions breed selfish acts, while religious insights and spiritual motivations inspire unselfish acts of social service and altruistic benevolence. [1]

In early conflict between egoism and altruism, the Thought Adjuster places preference on the latter for the goal of human happiness and the joys of the kingdom of heaven. [2] Egotism is vainglorious and suicidal, hindering intellectual cross-fertilization and ethical awakening. [3] Only after experiencing the sorrow and disillusionment of selfish pursuits will individuals be open to embracing the gospel of the kingdom. [4] Indulging the satisfactions of selfishness now may lead to exclusion from the kingdom in the future. [5]

Selfishness guarantees the downfall of the highest type of government, as it reflects the moral values of citizens and their chosen leaders, outweighing the political form of the state. [6]

Selfishness leads to darkness, death, and grief, but seeking God's goodness can deliver us from captivity and offer renewed opportunities for wiser living. [7]

Despite receiving patient instruction and loving warnings about their critical tendencies and self-importance, Lucifer and Caligastia wrongly judged their well-meaning advisers based on their own growing selfishness. [8] Man can reject survival by selfishly refusing to trust the divine pilot guiding the ascending soul to eternal salvation. [9] Selfishness may thwart the natural and instinctive bond between mother and child. [10]

Profit-driven economics is doomed without a balance of profit and service motives, as ruthless competition based on narrow-minded self-interest is ultimately destructive. [11]

Nonreligious activities distort the universe for selfish gain, while true religious individuals align themselves with the universe for the benefit of all beings. [12] Jesus portrayed the folly and sin of prostituting divine talents for selfish gain, as Lucifer and Caligastia did. [13] Unbridled self-will and unregulated self-expression culminate in unmitigated selfishness, the epitome of ungodliness. [14]

The universal economy thrives on motion and progress, reminding us that the universe is not egocentric and that we must recognize our responsibility to serve others. [15] The reward of weariness awaits those who ignore their brothers in the flesh, while pure hearts shall see God. [16]

The attempt to secure equal good for the self and for the greatest number of other selves presents a problem which cannot always be satisfactorily resolved in a time-space frame, as whoever would save his life shall lose it. [17] Purely material and wholly self-centered prayers do not find a place in the spirit circuit of the Eternal Son. [18]

Spiritual deliverance from selfishness

Jesus portrayed conquest by sacrifice, showing mercy to portray spiritual deliverance from all grudges, grievances, anger, and the lust for selfish power and revenge. [19]

Avoiding selfishness contributes to the economy of spiritual growth by fostering religious habits that cultivate sensitivity to divine values and encourage reflection on cosmic meanings. [20] It is important to be exposed to a vast number of other religious experiences to prevent egocentricity in your spiritual life. [21] Forsaking the self-life is essential to choosing the divine life and embracing true righteousness. [22] To transcend personal interests, meditate in God's sovereign presence to understand divine meanings and spiritual values. [23] Rest in the unassailable citadel of the spirit when the flood tides of selfishness and adversity assail the mortal soul. [24]
