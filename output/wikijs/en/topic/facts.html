Only facts and truth court the full light of comprehension and rejoice in the illumination and enlightenment of scientific research. [1] Humor functions to lessen the shock of the unexpected impact of fact or of truth, rigid unyielding fact and flexible ever-living truth. [2]

A human being can find truth in his inner experience, but he needs a clear knowledge of facts to apply his personal discovery of truth to the ruthlessly practical demands of everyday life. [3] Facts never quarrel with real spiritual faith; theories may. [4] We should seek truth as well as fact, expand our soul as well as our mind. [5] One can be technically right as to fact and everlastingly wrong in the truth. [6]
