During the dark ages, the church's alliance with politics led to a decline in spirituality and the rise of monastic orders. [1]

Peculiar and celibate orders of men and women arose, maintained by individuals lacking normal sex urges, as puberty advanced as the common age of marriage with the progress of civilization. [2]
