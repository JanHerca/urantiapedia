The Children of Israel were named after the days of Abraham. [1] Israel conspired with Egypt to refuse to pay tribute to Assyria, leading to their destruction. [2]

Israel's tumultuous history under various dynasties culminated in the rule of city despots who exploited land trade, leading to a significant shift in power dynamics. [3]

Israel's wars with Judah marked a tumultuous period of shifting powers and alliances, culminating in a decisive battle at Karkar against Assyrian forces. [4]
