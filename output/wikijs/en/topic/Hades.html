The Greeks believed that weak men must have weak souls; so they invented Hades as a fit place for the reception of such anemic souls; these unrobust specimens were also supposed to have shorter shadows. [1] In John the Baptist's parable about the rich man and the poor man, the rich man was tormented in Hades. [2]
