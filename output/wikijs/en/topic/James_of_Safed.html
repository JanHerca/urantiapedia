James of Safed was a citizen of Tiberias who had a fourteen-year-old son who was afflicted with epilepsy, and who was actually possessed by a malevolent midwayer. Jesus healed the boy. [1] James is the one who uttered to Jesus the famous phrase that says "Lord, I believe; help my unbelief". [2]
