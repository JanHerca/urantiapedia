In the treetops, mid-mammal ancestors birthed handsome twins, male and female, adapted to a warm climate with minimal body hair. [1] No two snow crystals are exactly alike, just as no two twins are alike. [2] The twin Primate ancestors quickly became the next vital step in prehuman evolution, standing upright from birth and communicating in new symbols. [3]

Primitive tribes often killed deformed or sickly children, and twins were believed to be caused by magic or infidelity, resulting in one usually being killed. [4] Twins were once regarded as very lucky or very unlucky, as early man feared and revered all unusual persons and beings. [5] In the treetop abode of simian ancestors, twins were born - a male and a female, who were handsome and lived comfortably in a warm climate. [6]
