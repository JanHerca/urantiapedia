If mortal man fails to survive natural death, the spirit content of any value is imperishable, and the surviving Adjuster conserves those realities born of love and nurtured in service. [1] Those who reject the eternal journey are moving counter to the stream of universe events. [2]

When nonsurvivors reject the ascendant life and choose against the Havona adventure, death results in their probationary careers ending, leaving them as if they had never been. [3] The Supreme's divinity expression is delayed by the choices of nonsurvivors in the grand universe. [4]

The eternal survival of souls is never hindered by neglect from the Adjuster; they faithfully fulfill their duties even after the mortals fail to achieve it. [5]

The final divergence of personality from the trend of the universe leads to intellectual isolation and spiritual cessation, highlighting the misadaptation of self-conscious life and the existence of a purposeful universe guided by a divine Creator. [6]

Guardian angels respond to the dispensational roll call for sleeping survivors, while Adjusters make no answer for nonsurvivors who have ceased to exist. [7] As nonsurvivors, can you allow yourself to pass into the realm of the unrealized when before you lies the enchanting vista of the universe career? [8] The personalities of nonsurvivors are absorbed into the Supreme, never to appear again as an individual creature. [9] The cosmic self-destruction is inevitable if evolving personalities are to have true freedom in exercising finite will. [10] Full adjudication of universe status follows identification with evil before penalty is executed. [11] By embracing God, man moves towards eternal reality; by withdrawing, he approaches nonreality and eventual cessation of existence. [12]
