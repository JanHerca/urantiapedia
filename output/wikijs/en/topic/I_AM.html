The concept of the I AM

The I AM is the hypothesis of all that is unknowable of God the Father, the Prime Source-Center; even this hypothesis surely falls far short of his unfathomable infinity. [1] It is the original infinity that can be hypothesized at the time of eternity, when as yet there was no differentiation in all infinity. [2]

The I AM is a theoretical and philosophical concession made by the celestial creatures to finite-minded men, who need time to understand relationships and causes. [3]

The I AM may be conceptually viewed as a unity, the solitary I AM; as a duality, the Infinite One differentiated from Infinity; as a triunity; and as a being of a sevenfold nature. [4] It is regarded as a philosophical postulate that the I AM pervades all infinity but no creature has been able to verify this by experience. [5]

The concept of the I AM implies complete self-realization, it embraces all the personalities who will have participated in the self-revelation of the I AM, his ultimate children. [6] The I AM contains both the past and the future, and therefore I AM also means I WAS and I WILL BE. [7] The Father revealed himself to Moses as I AM and as I AM that I AM; Jesus made an expanded revelation of the Father. [8]

In Buddhism the belief in many Buddhas led to the concept of the Eternal Buddha, a concept that can be identified with the Absolute and sometimes even with the infinite I AM. [9]

In the distant past

With his first action the I AM became the Eternal Father of the Original Son, and at the same time the Eternal Source of the Isle of Paradise. [10] The I AM exists before the existentials and after all the experientials. [11] The three spiritual expressions were perfectly unified in the I AM before he became the Universal Father of the Eternal Son and the Infinite Spirit. [12]

In the Deity of the Supreme the Father-I AM has achieved a relatively complete liberation from the limitations inherent in the infinite state, eternal existence, and absolute nature. [13]

The distant future

The final unification of the divergence between the interpretations of science, which is centered on energy, and those of religion, which is centered on spirit, occurs in the I AM. [14]

The personality of the I AM is partially experiential on spiritual levels even by mortals since it is in the Universal Father and in his revelation, the Eternal Son. [15] The realization of the Trinity of Trinities is thought to cause the I AM to become experientially attainable. [16]

The integration of total reality would occur in the union of the three Absolutes, the Unqualified, the Universal, and the Deity, which form the latent state of the I AM. [17]

The primary cause

The absolute original causality in infinity is attributed to the Universal Father, acting as the infinite, eternal, and absolute I AM. [18] All reality has its origin in the I AM. [19] He is the only self-caused fact in the universe; only the I AM is uncaused. [20] The I AM is also the Father of Michael of Nebadon and the God of human salvation. [21] The I AM is the primal cause and source of both the Trinity and the Absolute. [22] The I AM is Infinity, the eternal continuum. [23] The Original is the original manifestation of the I AM from which all reality springs. [24] The Unqualified Absolute is the unrevealed cosmic infinity of the I AM. [25]
