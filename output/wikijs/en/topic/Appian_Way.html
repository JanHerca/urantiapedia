In the time of Jesus, the Appian Way was a vital route for travel to Rome. Travelers like Gonod and Ganid used it to walk leisurely across Italy, encountering diverse people, including Roman citizens, Greek colonists, and descendants of slaves, while pack animals carried their belongings. [1]
