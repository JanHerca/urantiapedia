Adanson was the first child of Adam and Eve on Urantia. [1] Along with a company of 27 relatives Adanson migrated north from the second Garden. [2] His first wife and his 32 children went to Edentia after the absence of Adam and Eve. [3] Adanson founded a new civilization around 35,000 BC. [4] He grew wheat and barley. [5] Lived 396 years. [6] Traditions about Adanson survived in Greece. [7]

Adanson and Ratta

They had 67 sons and daughters; every fourth child was often invisible. [8] Were grandparents of almost 2,000 secondary intermediate. [9] Adanson and Ratta were married when Adanson was 120 years old. [10] Sato was a direct descendant of Adanson, and he and his town of tall people were the ancestors of the Greeks. [11]
