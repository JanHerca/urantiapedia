Trilobites, the dominant living creatures, were sexed animals, some with aborted eyes, that existed in many forms and included mud eaters. [1] The trilobites were the predecessors of crustaceans but nearly disappeared, giving way to larger mollusks and cephalopods in dominating the seas. [2]
