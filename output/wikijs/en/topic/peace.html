Social peace

Among religionists

We have persistently sought peace, but by rejecting the truth of God and aligning themselves on the side of error and darkness, the leaders of Israel ensure that peace cannot exist between light and darkness, truth and error. [1] Division and turmoil result when men reject the gospel of peace and good will preached by Jesus. [2] Jesus did not despise individuals, but recognized the inevitable clash between the religion of spirit and authority. [3] True peace among religions can only exist when all recognize the spirit sovereignty of God and relinquish claims to exclusive religious authority. [4] The time has come for a clear and open break with the religious rulers in Jerusalem. [5]

“Woe upon you, scribes, Pharisees, hypocrites, for rejecting truth and spurning mercy, for seeking honor among men while neglecting justice, and for persecuting the messengers of heaven.”. [6] Promote peace among various groups of believers to dedicate your life to brotherly love and unity, even in times of testing and separation. [7]

Among materialists

The first treaties of peace were sealed with blood and maidens, uniting tribes through rituals of respect and reconciliation. [8] Peace is the measure of civilization's advancement amidst the natural state of war and hostility in evolving man. [9] Primitive man could not long live in peace. [10] Peace on an evolutionary world is secured only by a social regulative system, as government compels the coordination of antagonisms within society. [11] Strive to live peaceably with all men, displaying wisdom, expertly ironing out disagreements, and loving their Father in heaven more. [12] The yellow race's social value of peace contributed to their population growth and the spread of their civilization. [13] Peace, not a natural tendency, derives from revelation and the teachings of Jesus, the Prince of Peace. [14]

Peace will prevail when man recognizes it as essential for his well-being and alternative means of releasing pent-up emotions have been established by society. [15] Without God, neither freedom, liberty, property, nor wealth leads to peace. [16] Spiritual unity does not require uniformity but instead celebrates the diversity of free personalities and spiritual originality. [17]

Spiritual peace

Survival is not fostered by seeking peace at any price, but by asserting the triumph of truth over evil. [18]

Religious living exhibits cosmic poise, a sublime peace that surpasses human understanding and signifies the absence of doubt and turmoil, akin to the unwavering faith of the Apostle Paul. [19]

The dove is a symbol of peace, and insects and animals were once worshipped due to a misinterpretation of the golden rule, representing a forgotten god or cult. [20] The peace which filled Jesus' soul on earth surpasses any stoicism or optimism in living a God-knowing life in the flesh. [21] Jesus gives to each all he will receive, peace I leave with you, my peace I give to you. [22] Jesus' peace is transformative, integrating personal and social harmony while preventing ruinous conflicts and fostering trust and unity. [23] Peace eludes the wicked, who are tormented by their unfulfilled ambitions, wounded pride, and troubled hearts. [24] Peace is not founded on fatalism or optimism, but rather on doing the will of God. [25] Take upon you the divine yoke and you will experience the peace of God, which passes all understanding. [26] Seek not for false peace but rather for the assurance of faith and the sureties of divine sonship for supreme joy in the spirit. [27] To a God-knowing believer, earthly crashes hold no significance; spiritual sureties are unassailable. [28]

Attained

Peace, as fruit of the Spirit, is manifested in the lives of those who are guided by the divine Spirit, yielding loving service, unselfish devotion, courageous loyalty, sincere fairness, enlightened honesty, undying hope, confiding trust, merciful ministry, unfailing goodness, forgiving tolerance, and enduring peace. [29] Through absolute faith in Father’s overcare, the peace of Jesus bestows joy and satisfaction to those who fully do the will of God in mortal life. [30]

The unity and survival of the soul are achieved by steadfastly asserting the triumph of truth over evil, rather than compromising noble aspirations for temporary peace of mind. [31] To ensure progress and true peace, fall wholeheartedly in love with truth and the ideals of eternal realities. [32] Keep your mind stayed on God to find true peace and avoid being led astray by false religious teachings. [33]

Achieving lasting joy and peace that surpasses understanding is possible by spiritual growth through unifying the soul powers of personality with love. [34]

Through misunderstood suffering, Job ascended to the superhuman plane of moral understanding and spiritual insight, ultimately obtaining the salvation of a vision of God. [35] Peace is found in salvation by faith, cleansing oneself from evil while seeking perfection in the love of God. [36] Only in consecrated living can we achieve peace, survival, perfection, and service, by choosing to submit our will to the Father's will. [37]

World peace

The attainment of peace is achieved through social fraternity, intellectual cross-fertilization, ethical awakening, political wisdom, and spiritual insight. [38]

Lasting peace on Urantia requires surrendering sovereign powers to mankind government, for treaties, diplomacy, and balances of power cannot maintain it. [39] Peace and war require leadership, not debate. [40] The lack of a common language impedes the extension of peace and must be overcome for global cultural unity. [41] Nationalism is the chief barrier to world peace. [42] International trade organizations are essential for promoting peace on Earth through social fraternity and cultural exchange. [43] The post-Adamic epoch heralds the age of internationalism, where race blending and a common language pave the way for universal peace and cooperation. [44] International trade organizations will promote peace on Urantia more effectively than sentimental peace planning. [45] World-wide peace under law will only be achieved when nations surrender power to make war to a representative government of all mankind. [46]
