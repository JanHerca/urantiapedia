The Iroquois federation was the closest the American red men came to achieving a state, but they lacked essential elements of modern national life and ultimately did not survive. [1] The Iroquois' adherence to the mother-family structure prevented them from achieving statehood. [2] The Iroquois reformed funeral waste by conserving property, enabling them to become the most powerful among northern red men. [3]
