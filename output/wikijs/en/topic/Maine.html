During the Silurian age, volcanic rocks and lava were found in eastern Maine, southern Europe, and Quebec. Volcanic activity had largely ceased, and it was a period of significant water deposition with minimal mountain building. During the ice age, the climate away from the ice remained mild, and the coastal climate varied greatly. Enormous icebergs would sometimes slide off the coast of Maine into the Atlantic. [1]
