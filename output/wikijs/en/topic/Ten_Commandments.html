The two versions of the Ten Commandments reflect the evolving understanding of divine law and the changing concepts of divinity. [1] Jesus wrote the Ten Commandments in Greek on two boards, which were later destroyed by him. [2] The taboos of Yahweh, promulgated by Moses, replaced thousands of pre-existent prohibitions and promised rewards for obedience. [3] God's Ten Commandments, recorded by Ganid, bring great peace to those who love his law. [4]
