Ignorance breeds suspicion, hindering intellectual cross-fertilization essential for brotherhood. [1]

Ignorance breeds suspicion, which is incompatible with sympathy and love, hindering intellectual cross-fertilization and brotherhood among primitive inhabitants. [2] Suspicion is the inherent reaction of primitive men, but trust is a new human acquisition instilled by the planetary seraphim of the Adamic regime. [3] Judas indulged in hate and suspicion, ultimately leading him to betray his friends and his Master. [4] Suspicion, a mental poison, tremendously interferes with spiritual progress, defiling the earthly tabernacle of the marvelous gift from God. [5] Teach children to trust by guiding them to choose heroes and lifework wisely, and refraining from suggesting suspicion. [6] Stumble not into misunderstanding entanglements of suspicion, but love all men as brothers in the household of faith. [7]
