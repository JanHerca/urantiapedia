Jesus, the red-blooded Galilean fishermen called Master, was a strong and forceful yet gentle and unassuming intellectual and spiritual personality. [1]

Among Zebedee's numerous employees in Capernaum, Jesus was first known as “Master” during the weekly meetings with the household, shop, and shore helpers who deeply loved him, even though he missed the children in the carpenter shop of Nazareth. [2]
