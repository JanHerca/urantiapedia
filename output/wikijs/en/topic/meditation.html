Jesus taught his followers that after prayer, they should remain in silent receptivity to allow the indwelling spirit to speak to the listening soul, transforming them to resemble the being they worship. [1]

Communicating with the spirit of your own soul through meditation is always successful, even if the experience remains as a superconscious registration. [2] Meditation on God brings deliverance from the illusions of evil and ultimate salvation from all material fetters. [3] Deeper connection with God is achieved through selfless service rather than solitary meditation alone. [4] Reflective meditation on cosmic meanings favors spiritual growth. [5]

Spiritual meditation is reflective worship and prayer of thanksgiving, not to be confused with mystical experiences favored by factors indicative of danger. [6] Learn the value of meditation through intelligent reflection. [7]

Meditation makes contact of mind with spirit, allowing for the interchange of strength for weakness and the will of God for the mind of self, ultimately leading to worship as the philosopher views it. [8]

Man can never wisely decide temporal issues or transcend selfishness without meditating in the presence of God's sovereignty and reckoning with divine meanings and spiritual values. [9] Modern man thinks he is too busy for spiritual meditation, forsaking real values and spiritual satisfactions. [10] To find God in all things, one must meditate on eternal realities. [11] The difficult and time-consuming practice of meditation ultimately leads to the formation of a mature spiritual character. [12] Prolonged isolation of personality is most undesirable in the habitual practice of religious daydreaming. [13]

The value in problem solving through worshipful meditation, as taught by Jesus, is the ultimate method for gathering wisdom and energy to solve moral and spiritual dilemmas. [14]
