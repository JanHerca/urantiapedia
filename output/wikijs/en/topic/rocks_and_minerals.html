Boulders, the distinctive evidence of glacial action, stand as silent witnesses to the powerful force of ice in reshaping the earth's surface. [1] The Cambrian layers overthrust the Cretaceous in British Columbia during crustal distortions in the Rocky Mountain region 70,000,000 years ago. [2]

The Mithraic cult portrayed a militant god with origins in a great rock, engaging in valiant exploits and causing water to gush forth from a struck rock. [3]

The older, water-deposited rocks with fossil remains are often commingled with younger layers, representing the continual flux of the early crust of the earth during the Proterozoic era. [4] Rock flour, a glacial product, is a distinctive evidence of the ice's transformative impact on the topography. [5] A list of rocks and minerals named in The Urantia Book is in the links.
