Cardinal virtues of Socrates were wisdom, courage, temperance, and justice. [1] Carried to extreme, virtue may become vice. [2] Is righteousness; conformity with the cosmos. [3] Is volitional. [4] Morality is virtue. [5] Not truly acquired until acts make one worthy of it. [6] Proportion required in exercise of. [7] Realized by consistent choosing of good over evil. [8] Supreme virtue is wholeheartedly to do Father’s will. [9]
