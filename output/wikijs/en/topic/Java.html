During the Andonic migrations, a continuous land path from England to Java connected Europe and Asia. [1] Asoka established Buddhism in Java, where it soon became the dominant religion in half the world. [2] 950,000 years ago, descendants of Andon and Fonta crossed the land bridge connecting Java to Asia. [3]
