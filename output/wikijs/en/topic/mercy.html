Definition

Mercy, the wise ministry of the universe Father, is the natural and inevitable offspring of goodness and love. [4] The culmination of justice, fairness, and patience in mercy ministry leads to genuine acts of mercy as a beautiful climax to spiritual growth. [5] Mercy, correlated in personality, reveals divinity comprehensible as truth, beauty, and goodness. [6] Mercy is the personal expression of righteousness, a harmonious blend of love and justice in the Trinity's divine operation. [7]

Mercy is God's loving-kindness and compassion towards each individual, a fairness technique of adjustment between perfection and imperfection in the evolving universe. [8] God's justice is always tempered with mercy, for “mercy is simply justice tempered by that wisdom which grows out of perfection of knowledge.”. [9] We should show mercy to all, leading to perfection, as your Father in heaven does. [10] Mercy is a quality of growth, learned through spirit ethics and applied by ascending creatures in genuine acts of kindness, patience, and true mercy. [11]

Divine mercy creates a saving interval between seedtime and harvest, allowing for the testing of character and repentance, demonstrating that God is love. [12] Evil deeds lead to sorrow and pain, but there is a season of grace before the full ripening of consequences. [13] Jesus taught that by showing mercy and forgiving others, we can achieve spiritual deliverance from anger, selfish motives, and the desire for revenge. [14]

Mercy ministry

The Eternal Son is the embodiment of mercy, the manifestation of divine love and endless mercy to all creation. [15]

Evidence that man is indwelt by God is found in the manifestation of divine mercy and tenderness towards the weak and needy, showing the unique blending of human and divine in preparation for eternal universe service. [16]

Showing mercy to a wrongdoer within a family can temporarily burden other family members, but ultimately, each member's actions have a ripple effect on the group as a whole. [17] Forgive not only seven times, but even to seventy times seven, as the kingdom of heaven is likened to a certain king who showed mercy and forgiveness. [18]

In cases of doubt, universe governments will advance a soul to higher status for the individual's benefit, ensuring divine justice and further opportunities for mercy. [19] Decisions should favor those in need, with merciful regard for the suffering victims of unfortunate circumstances. [20]

The merciful shall obtain mercy through loving-kindness and supreme fatherliness, as justice in the universe cannot be circumvented by even the infinite love of God. [21] Mercy credits, of lavish proportions, are established to insure the survival of every soul who desires divine citizenship. [22]

Mercy ministry is always the work of the individual, while justice punishment is the function of social, governmental, or universe administrative groups. [23]

The Memory of Mercy must be fully paid out in the ministry of Third Source and Center personalities, not thrust upon those who despise it or cast pearls before swine. [24]

The seraphic court advisers act as defenders of mortals, ensuring that every misstep is fairly adjudged in accordance with creature nature and divine purpose. [25]

In the vicissitudes of life, love one another, do not strive with men, show mercy to those who abuse you, and be upright artisans, loyal citizens, praiseworthy neighbors, devoted kinsmen, understanding parents, and sincere believers in the brotherhood of the Father's kingdom. [26]

Cultivating religious habits that foster a sensitive spiritual reflex, recognition of divine values in others, and a humble refusal to presume on divine mercy are key to unconscious spiritual growth. [27] The time lag of mercy allows for repentance and rehabilitation, demonstrating the wisdom of delay in the execution of justice in the universe. [28]

When the provisions of endless mercy and nameless patience have been exhausted, justice and righteousness will prevail, ultimately annihilating that which mercy cannot rehabilitate. [29]
