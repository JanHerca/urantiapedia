The Adjuster, Holy Spirit, and Spirit of Truth enable mortals to truly recognize their sonship with God. [1]

Full function of personality is the starting point for realizing sonship with God, as a selfhood indwelt by a fragment of God the Father discloses the capacity for receiving divine presence and responding to the gravitational pull of the Paradise Father. [2] Only through spiritual insight can man break the fetters of mortal status and find true sonship with God. [3]

Through acceptance of sonship, belief in our spiritual promise, and the indwelling of the Father's fragment, mortals become sons of God in eternal reality, united with Christ and drawn toward the Universal Father. [4]

The key to survival is the willingness to accept sonship and cooperate with the indwelling Adjuster, leading to comprehension and eventual passage to Paradise. [5] Sonship with God does not negate citizenship in earthly governments; believers are called to be exemplary citizens in both realms. [6]

In the final analysis, the destinies of the ascending orders of sonship cannot be labeled as “greater” or “lesser”, as every son of God shares the fatherhood of God and is loved by him equally, making sonship the supreme relationship of the creature to the Creator. [7] To have fellowship with God, assume the obligations of sonship and be willing to make the supreme sacrifice in love. [8]

How sonship is obtained

By faith and freewill co-operation with the Adjuster, you can attain the spiritual status of ascending sonship and become one of the ascending Sons of God. [9] God's will becoming our will is the path to true sonship with God. [10] Sonship with God is received by grace and through faith, not earned. [11] Loving neighbor as self is the supreme duty of man, expressing sonship with God. [12]

Receiving God as Father ensures our secure position in the kingdom of divine sonship, witnessing the genuineness of our message through the fruits of the spirit. [13] Nicodemus was accomplished and altruistic, yet his lack of submission to Father's will left him bewildered and unfulfilled in sonship with God. [14]
