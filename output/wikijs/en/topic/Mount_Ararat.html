Mount Ararat, akin to Mount Sinai for the Hebrews, was the sacred mountain of the Vanites, with traditions dating back ten thousand years. [1] Mount Ararat is the sacred Vanite mountain in northern Mesopotamia, where ancient traditions and moral laws were believed to have originated. [2]
