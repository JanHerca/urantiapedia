In the court of gentiles, Jesus was shocked by the irreverence of the money-changers and vendors in his Father's house. [1] Jesus cleansed the temple from money-changers profiting from commercializing religious practices to protect the poor from unfair profiteering. [2] Licensed money-changers exchanged various currencies for shekels in the temple precincts, profiting from temple dues and commercial transactions. [3]
