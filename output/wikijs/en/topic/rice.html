The enforced changes in living conditions caused Adamites to become omnivorous by adding rice and other foods to their diet, leading to improved health and vigor. [1]

Rice, thrown on newlyweds to ensure fecundity, was a magical rite observed by many Near Eastern peoples on lucky days like Thursdays and during weddings celebrated at the full moon. [2]
