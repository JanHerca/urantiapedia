10 million years ago, during the last great global migration, bears from Asia entered North America via land bridges, while other species moved between continents. Later, during the glacial period 100,000 years ago, bears demonstrated remarkable adaptability by following the advancing and retreating glaciers. Alongside species like reindeer and mammoths, they endured extreme conditions, solidifying their status as one of the most resilient mammals of the era. [1]
