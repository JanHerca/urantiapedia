The Adansonites were the discoverers of bronze. [1] The Adansonite seat was east of the southern shore of the Caspian Sea, near the Kopet Mountains. [2] The Adansonites, led by Sato, a direct descendant of Adamson and Ratta, settled in Greece in 10,000 BC, and formed the ancestors of the Greeks. [3]

The Adansonites are the descendants of Adamson, son of Adam and Eve, and Ratta, a surviving Nodite. They maintained a high culture for 7,000 years. They mixed with Nodites and Andonites but their culture survived into later European civilization. [4] Together with the Adamites they formed the violet race, one of the nine pure races that have existed on Earth. [5]
