Jesus rejected the disappointment of reign like Judas Maccabee, choosing loyalty to his Father's will above all. [1] The Jewish people were vibrant with expectation due to Judas Maccabee's heroic exploits, hoping for the appearance of the Messiah. [2] During the times of Judas Maccabee, Jews were removed from Perea, leaving the region equally gentile and Jewish. [3]

After meeting John and learning about the history of Israel, Jesus expressed a keen interest in the meaning of Sabbath rites and commemorations, including the festive illumination marking the temple's dedication after Judas Maccabee restored the Mosaic services. [4]
