<p style="text-align:center;">
<i>A little tribute</i><br>
<i>To us, women.</i><br>
<i>Of all ages and nationalities,</i><br>
<i>And especially to all those who,</i><br>
<i>Against all odds, persevere in loving,</i><br>
<i>to dream and fight for a better world.</i><br>
<i>The musical theme of Vangelis,</i><br>
<i>Greek composer,</i><br>
<i>It is called precisely</i><br>
<i>“Glorianna — Hymn to women”.</i><br>
<i>And God made me a woman,</i><br>
<i>With long hair,</i><br>
<i>The eyes, the nose</i><br>
<i>And the woman's mouth,</i><br>
<i>With curves and folds</i><br>
<i>And sweet hollows;</i><br>
<i>From the inside he dug me,</i><br>
<i>And made me</i><br>
<i>The workshop of human beings.</i><br>
<i>He delicately wove my nerves,</i><br>
<i>Balanced with care</i><br>
<i>The number of my hormones,</i><br>
<i>Composed my blood</i><br>
<i>And injected it into me</i><br>
<i>So that it irrigates</i><br>
<i>My whole body;</i><br>
<i>Thus ideas were born,</i><br>
<i>Dreams and instinct.</i><br>
<i>He created everything</i><br>
<i>With great bursts of breath</i><br>
<i>By sculpting with love,</i><br>
<i>A thousand and one things</i><br>
<i>Who make me a woman every day,</i><br>
<i>And for which with pride,</i><br>
<i>I get up every morning</i><br>
<i>And bless my sex.</i><br>
</p>

Nicaraguan poetess

Gioconda Belli
