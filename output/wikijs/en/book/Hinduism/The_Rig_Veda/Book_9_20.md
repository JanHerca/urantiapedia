## HYMN XI Soma Pavamana.

<p id="v1"><sup><small>1</small></sup>  SING forth to Indu, O ye men, to him who is purified,
Fain to pay worship to the Gods.
<p id="v2"><sup><small>2</small></sup>  Together with thy pleasant juice the Atharvans have commingled milk,
Divine, devoted to the God.
<p id="v3"><sup><small>3</small></sup>  Bring, by thy flowing, weal to kine, weal to the people, weal to steeds.
Weal, O thou King, to growing plants
<p id="v4"><sup><small>4</small></sup>  Sing a praise-song to Soma brown of hue, of independent might.
The Red, who reaches up to heaven.
<p id="v5"><sup><small>5</small></sup>  Purify Soma when effused with stones which bands move rapidly,
And pour the sweet milk in the meath.
<p id="v6"><sup><small>6</small></sup>  With humble homage draw ye nigh; blend the libation with the curds:
To Indra offer Indu up.
<p id="v7"><sup><small>7</small></sup>  Soma, foe-que chief o’er men, doing the will of pour forth
Prosperity upon our kine.
<p id="v8"><sup><small>8</small></sup>  Heart-knower, Sovran of the heart, thou art effused, O Soma, that Indra may drink thee and rejoice.
<p id="v9"><sup><small>9</small></sup>  O Soma Pavamana, give us riches and heroic strength,—
Indu! with. Indra for ally.

## HYMN XII. Soma Pavamana.

<p id="v1"><sup><small>1</small></sup>  To Indra have the Soma drops, exceeding rich in sweets, been poured,
Shed in the seat of sacrifice.
<p id="v2"><sup><small>2</small></sup>  As mother kine low to their calves, to Indra have the sages called,
Called him to drink the Soma juice.
<p id="v3"><sup><small>3</small></sup>  In the stream's wave wise Soma dwells, distilling rapture, in his seat,
Resting upon a wiId-cow's hide.
<p id="v4"><sup><small>4</small></sup>  Far-sighted Soma, Sage and Seer, is worshipped in the central point
Of heaven, the straining-cloth of wool.
<p id="v5"><sup><small>5</small></sup>  In close embraces Indu holds Soma when
poured within the jars.
And on the. purifying sieve.
<p id="v6"><sup><small>6</small></sup>  Indu sends forth a voice on high to regions of the sea of air,
Shaking the vase that drops with meath.
<p id="v7"><sup><small>7</small></sup>  The Tree whose praises never fail yields heavenly milk among our hymns,
Urging men's generations on.
<p id="v8"><sup><small>8</small></sup>  The Wise One, with the Sage's stream, the Soma urged to speed, flows on
To the dear places of the sky.
<p id="v9"><sup><small>9</small></sup>  O Pavamana, bring us wealth bright with a thousand splendours. Yea.
O Indu, give us ready help.

## HYMN XIII. Soma Pavamana.

<p id="v1"><sup><small>1</small></sup>  PASSED through, the fleece in thousand streams the Soma, purified, flows on
To Indra's, Viyu's special place.
<p id="v2"><sup><small>2</small></sup>  Sing forth, ye men who long for help, to Pavamana, to the Sage,
Effused to entertain the Gods.
<p id="v3"><sup><small>3</small></sup>  The Soma-drops with thousand powers are purified for victory,
Hymned to become the feast of Gods.
<p id="v4"><sup><small>4</small></sup>  Yea, as thou flowest bring great store of food that we may win the spoil
Indu, bring splendid manly might.
<p id="v5"><sup><small>5</small></sup>  May they in flowing give us wealth in thousands, and heroic power,—
These Godlike Soma-drops effused.
<p id="v6"><sup><small>6</small></sup>  Like coursers by their drivers urged, they were poured forth, for victory,
Swift through the woollen straining-cloth.
<p id="v7"><sup><small>7</small></sup>  Noisily flow the Soma-drops, like milch-kine lowing to their calves:
They have run forth from both the hands.
<p id="v8"><sup><small>8</small></sup>  As Gladdener whom Indra loves, O Pavamana, with a roar
Drive all our enemies away.
<p id="v9"><sup><small>9</small></sup>  O Pavamamas, driving off the godless, looking on the light,
Sit in the place of sacrifice.

## HYMN XIV. Soma Pavamana.

<p id="v1"><sup><small>1</small></sup>  REPOSING on the river's wave the Sage hath widely flowed around,
Bearing the hymn which many love.
<p id="v2"><sup><small>2</small></sup>  When the Five kindred Companies, active in duty, with the song
Establish him, the Powerful,
<p id="v3"><sup><small>3</small></sup>  Then in his juice whose strength is great, have all the Gods rejoiced themselves,
When he hath clothed him in the milk.
<p id="v4"><sup><small>4</small></sup>  Freeing himself he flows away, leaving his body's severed limbs,
And meets his own Companion here.
<p id="v5"><sup><small>5</small></sup>  He by the daughters of the priest, like a fair youth, hath been adorned,
Making the milk, as ’twere, his robe.
<p id="v6"><sup><small>6</small></sup>  O'er the fine fingers, through desire of milk, in winding course he goes,
And utters voice which he hath found.
<p id="v7"><sup><small>7</small></sup>  The nimble fingers have approached, adorning him the Lord of Strength:
They grasp the vigorous Courser's back.
<p id="v8"><sup><small>8</small></sup>  Comprising all the treasures that are in the heavens and on the earth,
Come, Soma, as our faithful Friend.

## HYMN XV. Soma Pavamana.

<p id="v1"><sup><small>1</small></sup>  THROUGH the fine fingers, with the song, this Hero comes with rapid ears,
Going to Indra's special place.
<p id="v2"><sup><small>2</small></sup>  In holy thought he ponders much for the great worship of the Gods.
Where the Immortals have their seat.
<p id="v3"><sup><small>3</small></sup>  Like a good horse is he led out, when on the path that shines with light
The mettled steeds exert their strength.
<p id="v4"><sup><small>4</small></sup>  He brandishes his horns on high, and whets them Bull who leads the herd,
Doing with might heroic deeds.
<p id="v5"><sup><small>5</small></sup>  He moves, a vigorous Steed, adorned with beauteous rays of shining gold,
Becoming Sovran of the streams.
<p id="v6"><sup><small>6</small></sup>  He, over places rough to pass, bringing rich treasures closely packed.
Descends into the reservoirs.
<p id="v7"><sup><small>7</small></sup>  Men beautify him in the vats, him worthy to be beautified,
Him who brings forth abundant food.
<p id="v8"><sup><small>8</small></sup>  Him, even him, the fingers ten and the seven songs make beautiful,
Well-weaponed, best of gladdeners.

## HYMN XVI. Soma Pavamana.

<p id="v1"><sup><small>1</small></sup>  THE pressers from the Soma-press send forth thy juice for rapturous joy
The speckled sap runs like a flood.
<p id="v2"><sup><small>2</small></sup>  With strength we follow through the sieve him who brings might and wins the kine,
Enrobed in water with his juice.
<p id="v3"><sup><small>3</small></sup>  Pour on the sieve the Soma, ne’er subdued in waters, waterless,
And make it pure for Indra's drink.
<p id="v4"><sup><small>4</small></sup>  Moved by the purifier's thought, the Soma flows into the sieve:
By wisdom it hath gained its home.
<p id="v5"><sup><small>5</small></sup>  With humble homage, Indra, have the Soma-drops flowed forth to thee,
Contending for the glorious prize.
<p id="v6"><sup><small>6</small></sup>  Purified in his fleecy garb, attaining every beauty, he
Stands, hero-like, amid the kine.
<p id="v7"><sup><small>7</small></sup>  Swelling, as ’twere, to heights of heaven, the stream of the creative juice
Falls lightly on the cleansing sieve.
<p id="v8"><sup><small>8</small></sup>  Thus, Soma, purifying himwho knoweth song mid living men,
Thou wanderest through the cloth of wool.

## HYMN XVII. Soma Pavamana.

<p id="v1"><sup><small>1</small></sup>  LIKE rivers down a steep descent, slaying the Vṛtras, full of zeal,
The rapid Soma-streams have flowed.
<p id="v2"><sup><small>2</small></sup>  The drops of Soma juice effused fall like the rain upon the earth:
To Indra flow the Soma-streams.
<p id="v3"><sup><small>3</small></sup>  With swelling wave the gladdening drink, the Soma, flows intoṭhe sieve,
Loving the Gods and slaying fiends.
<p id="v4"><sup><small>4</small></sup>  It hastens to the pitchers, poured upon the sieve it waxes strong
At sacrifices through the lauds.
<p id="v5"><sup><small>5</small></sup>  Soma, thou shinest mounting heaven as ’twere above light's triple realm,
And moving secm'st to speed the Sun.
<p id="v6"><sup><small>6</small></sup>  To him, the head of sacnfice, singers and bards have sung their songs,
Offering what he loves to see.
<p id="v7"><sup><small>7</small></sup>  The men, the sages with their hymns, eager for help, deck thee strong &teed,
Deck thee for service of the Gods.
<p id="v8"><sup><small>8</small></sup>  Flow onward to the stream of meath rest efficacious in thy home,
Fair, to be drunk at sacrifice.

## HYMN XVIII. Soma Pavamana.

<p id="v1"><sup><small>1</small></sup>  THOU, Soma, dweller on the hills, effused, hast flowed into the sieve,:
All-bounteous art thou in carouse.
<p id="v2"><sup><small>2</small></sup>  Thou art a sacred Bard, a Sage; the meath is offipring of thy sap:
All-bountcous art thou in carouse.
<p id="v3"><sup><small>3</small></sup>  All Deities of one accord have come that they may drink of thee:
All-bounteous art thou in carouse.
<p id="v4"><sup><small>4</small></sup>  He who containeth in his hands all treasures much to be desired:
All-bounteous art thou in carouse.
<p id="v5"><sup><small>5</small></sup>  Who milketh out this mighty Pair, the Earth and Heaven, like mother kine
All-bounteous art thou in carouse.
<p id="v6"><sup><small>6</small></sup>  Who in a moment mightily floweth around these two world-halvcs:
All-bounteous art thou in carouse.
<p id="v7"><sup><small>7</small></sup>  The Strong One, being purified, hath in the pitchers cried aloud:
All-bounteous art thou in carouse.

## HYMN XIX. Soma Pavamana.

<p id="v1"><sup><small>1</small></sup>  O SOMA, being purified bring us the wondrous treasure, meet
For lauds, that is in earth and heaven.
<p id="v2"><sup><small>2</small></sup>  For ye Twain, Indra, Soma, are Lords of the light, Lords of the kine:
Great Rulers, prosper ye our songs.
<p id="v3"><sup><small>3</small></sup>  The tawny Steer, while cleansed among the living, bellowing on the grass,
Hath sunk and settled in his home.
<p id="v4"><sup><small>4</small></sup>  Over the Steer's productive flow the sacred songs were resonant,
The mothers of the darling Son.
<p id="v5"><sup><small>5</small></sup>  Hath he not, purified, impregned the kine whb long to meet their Lord,
The kine who yield the shining milk?
<p id="v6"><sup><small>6</small></sup>  Bring near us those who stand aloof strike fear into our enemies:
O Pavamana, find us wealth.
<p id="v7"><sup><small>7</small></sup>  Soma, bring down the foeman's might, his vigorous strength and vital powe'r,
Whether he be afar or near.

## HYMN XX Soma Pavamana.

<p id="v1"><sup><small>1</small></sup>  FORTH through the straining-cloth the Sage flows to the banquet of the Gods,
Subduing all our enemies.
<p id="v2"><sup><small>2</small></sup>  For he, as Pavamana, sends thousandfold treasure in the shape
Of cattle to the singing-men.
<p id="v3"><sup><small>3</small></sup>  Thou graspest all things with thy mind, and purifiest thee with thoughts
As such, O Soma, find us fame.
<p id="v4"><sup><small>4</small></sup>  Pour lofty glory on us, send sure riches to our liberal lords,
Bring food to those who sing thy praise.
<p id="v5"><sup><small>5</small></sup>  As thou art cleansed, O Wondrous Steed, O Soma, thou hast entered, like
A pious King, into the songs.
<p id="v6"><sup><small>6</small></sup>  He, Soma, like a courser in the floods invincible, made clean
With hands, is resting in the jars.
<p id="v7"><sup><small>7</small></sup>  Disporting, like a liberal chief, thou goest, Soma, to the sieve,
Lending the laud a Hero's strength.
