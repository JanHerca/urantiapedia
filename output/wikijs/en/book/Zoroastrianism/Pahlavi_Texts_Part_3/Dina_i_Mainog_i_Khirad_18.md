## DÎNÂ-Î MAÎNÔG-Î KHIRA<i>D</i>. CHAPTER XVIII.

1\. The sage asked the spirit of wisdom (2) thus: ‘Wherefore do people consider these very little, <span id="p50"><sup><small>[ p. 50 ]</small></sup></span> these four things which it is necessary for them to consider more, as warnings (dakhshak), (3) the changeableness of the things of the worldly _existence_, the death of the body, the account of the soul [^1], _and_ the fear of hell?’

4\. The spirit of wisdom answered (5) thus: ‘On account of the delusiveness (nîyâ<i>z</i>ânîh) of the demon of greediness [^2], and of discontent.’
