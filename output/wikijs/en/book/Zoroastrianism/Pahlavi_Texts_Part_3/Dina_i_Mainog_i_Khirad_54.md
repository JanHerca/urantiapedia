## DÎNÂ-Î MAÎNÔG-Î KHIRA<i>D</i>. CHAPTER LIV.

1\. The sage asked the spirit of wisdom (2) thus: ‘Wherefore _is it_ when an ignorant man—when they bring advancement to him—considers the learning <span id="p97"><sup><small>[ p. 97 ]</small></sup></span> and advancement of the wise and good mostly so [^1], through greediness, that to teach _it_ to him is difficult?’

3\. The spirit of wisdom answered (4) thus: ‘For this reason, because the ignorant man considers, in thought, his own ignorance as good as the sage _does_, in thought, his own knowledge.’
