## DÎNKA<i>R</i><i>D</i>.—BOOK IX. CHAPTER VIII.

_Sû<i>d</i>kar Nask_.

1\. The seventh fargar<i>d</i>, Tâ-v<i>e</i>\-urvâtâ [^2], is about <span id="p181"><sup><small>[ p. 181 ]</small></sup></span> the exhibition to Zaratû<i>s</i>t of the nature of the four periods in the millennium of Zaratû<i>s</i>t [1](sbe37058_htm.html). 2. First, the golden, that in which Aûha<i>r</i>ma<i>z</i><i>d</i> displayed the religion to Zaratû<i>s</i>t. 3. Second, the silver, that in which Vi<i>s</i>tâsp [2](sbe37058_htm.html) received the religion from Zaratû<i>s</i>t. 4. Third, the steel, the period within which the organizer of righteousness, Âtû<i>r</i>pâ<i>d</i> [3](sbe37058_htm.html) son of Mâ<i>r</i>aspend, was born. 5. Fourth, the period mingled with iron is this, in which is much propagation of the authority of the apostate and other villains, as regards the destruction of the reign of religion, the weakening of every kind _of_ goodness _and_ virtue, and the disappearance of honour and wisdom from the countries of Irân. 6. In the same period is an account of the many perplexities and torments (zakhamî-hastanŏ) of the period for that desire of the life of the good which subsists in seemliness.

7\. Perfect righteousness is excellence.
