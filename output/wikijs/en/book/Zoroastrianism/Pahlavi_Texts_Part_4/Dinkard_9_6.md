## DÎNKA<i>R</i><i>D</i>.—BOOK IX. CHAPTER VI.

_Sû<i>d</i>kar Nask_.

1\. The fifth fargar<i>d</i>, Khshmaibyâ [^1], is about the forgetfulness of a father for a son, a son for a father, a brother for a brother, a friend for a friend, a husband (mânpatŏ) for a wife (nârîk), and a wife for a husband in a measurable time, through excess and festivity (khang); and the unforgetfulness of the spirit of the Gâthas for so many reciters and chanters of the Gâthas. 2. About the complaint of the spirit of the Gâthas when a high-priest, although priest of the country-folk (dehîgânŏ), passes away in an out-district [^2], and the body of that man does not come back to his own land; whatever is relating to that, _and_, besides that, what is to be born in that <span id="p179"><sup><small>[ p. 179 ]</small></sup></span> land, and the oppressiveness of apostates _which_ arises. 3. About the superior power of the spirit of the Gâthas, _and_ also that of liberality, in preserving the soul from hell.

4\. Excellence _that is_ perfect is righteousness.
