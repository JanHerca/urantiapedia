## DÎNKA<i>R</i><i>D</i>.—BOOK VIII. CHAPTER XI.

1\. The Vi<i>s</i>tâsp-sâstô [^1] is about particulars of <span id="p24"><sup><small>[ p. 24 ]</small></sup></span> every kind _relating_ to Kaî-Vi<i>s</i>tâsp [^2]; the temper, character, demeanour, knowledge, learning, and law for sovereignty; the government of the creatures, _and_ the advancement of the will of the sacred beings requisite for it.

2\. The creator Aûha<i>r</i>ma<i>z</i><i>d</i> sends the archangels [^3] on to Kai -Vi<i>s</i>tâsp as evidence about Aûha<i>r</i>ma<i>z</i><i>d</i>, _and_ a reminder of Spîtâmân Zaratû<i>s</i>t, _of_ the pure goodness of the Ma<i>z</i><i>d</i>a-worshipping religion, _and of_ the command for the ruler Vi<i>s</i>tâsp, as to _its_ triumph, on accepting the religion from Zaratû<i>s</i>t. 3. The visible coming of the archangels to the metropolis, and, secondly, _their_ domestication (handêmânîh) at the residence _of_ Vi<i>s</i>tâsp and his companions; the envoys’ explanation of Aûha<i>r</i>ma<i>z</i><i>d</i>'s message to Vi<i>s</i>tâsp, _and_ the accepting of the Ma<i>z</i><i>d</i>a-worshipping religion _by_ the obedient king Vi<i>s</i>tâsp.

4\. The outpouring (sârînî<i>d</i>anŏ) of Ar<i>g</i>âsp the Khyôn [^4], by the demon of wrath, for war with Vi<i>s</i>tâsp <span id="p25"><sup><small>[ p. 25 ]</small></sup></span> _and_ disturbance of Zaratû<i>s</i>t; the arrangements _and_ movements of king Vi<i>s</i>tâsp for that war, _and_ whatever is on the same subject.

5\. Excellence _that is_ perfect is righteousness.

## Footnotes

[^1]: (23:3) Corresponding to the tenth word, dazdâ, in the Ahunavair, according to B. P. Riv.; but it is the eleventh Nask in other Rivâyats. p. 24 Vi<i>s</i>tâsp-sâstô means ‘the instruction of Vi<i>s</i>tâsp,’ and is corrupted into Vi<i>s</i>tâsp-shâh, Vi<i>s</i>tâspâd, or Vi<i>s</i>tâsp, in the Rivâyats, which also state that it contained originally sixty kardah, or ʓûrat, of which only ten, or eight, were recovered after the time of Alexander. The last number refers, no doubt, to the eight fargar<i>d</i>s still extant under the corrupt name Vi<i>s</i>tâsp Ya<i>s</i>t, which probably consist of fragments of the Avesta text of this Nask; but in comparing that text with this description it must be remembered that the author is describing the contents of the Pahlavi version which would contain much commentary.

[^2]: (24:1) The king of Iran in the time of Zaratû<i>s</i>t, who accepted the Ma<i>z</i><i>d</i>a-worshipping religion; the last king of the old history derived from the Avesta (see Chap. XIII, 15, 16).

[^3]: (24:2) Compare Vi<i>s</i>tâsp Yt. 40.

[^4]: (24:3) The ‘deadly <i>H</i><i>v</i>yaona Are<i>g</i>a<i>d</i>\-aspa’ of Yt. IX, 30, XVII, 50, whom Kavi Vî<i>s</i>tâspa prayed to be delivered from. According to the Yâ<i>d</i>kâr-î Zarîrân, Ar<i>g</i>âsp, king of the Khyôns, made war upon p. 25 Vi<i>s</i>tâsp on account of the latter's conversion to Ma<i>z</i><i>d</i>a-worship and was defeated with great difficulty in a most desperate battle which is also described in the Shâhnâmah. Whether the Khyôns were the Chionitae of later times is uncertain.
