## DÎNKA<i>R</i><i>D</i>.—BOOK VIII. CHAPTER XII.

1\. The Avesta _and_ commentary of the Va<i>s</i>tag [^1] _have_ not reached us through any high-priest.

2\. Excellence _that_ is perfect _is_ righteousness.

## Footnotes

[^1]: (25:1) Corresponding to the eleventh word, mananghô, in the Ahunavair, according to B. P. Riv.; but it is the twelfth Nask in other Rivâyats. The name of this Nask is very uncertain; in five occurrences of the word the first letter is omitted once and may once be the conjunction ‘and,’ and the last syllable is also omitted once; the B. P. Riv. calls it Did, by omitting the first and last letters and varying the reading of the rest, and the other Rivâyats call it ‘<i>H</i>a<i>s</i>t or ‘Ha<i>s</i>t. They also state that it contained twenty-two kardah, or fargar<i>d</i>s, in six divisions treating of various religious and worldly duties, as detailed in the translations in the latter part of this volume.
