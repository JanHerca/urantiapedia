## DÎNKA<i>R</i><i>D</i>.—BOOK VIII. CHAPTER X.

1\. The Ka<i>s</i>kîsrôbô [^1] _contains_ particulars about the explanation of the ceremonial _and_ ritual of the sacred beings, through what _arises its_ conversion _into_ demon-worship, _and_ information _as to_ cleanness and uncleanness. 2. The preparations and precautions for the Ya<i>s</i>ts [^2]; the tokens and signs of the overflowing and evil owing to the demons at various times, and the cause of their exhaustion and the final victory of the sacred beings. 3. Then the exalting chants _of_ every kind, which Aûha<i>r</i>ma<i>z</i><i>d</i> _taught_ to Zaratû<i>s</i>t, are called the teaching (sâstô) of the spirits.

4\. Excellence _that is_ perfect is righteousness.

## Footnotes

[^1]: (23:1) Corresponding to the ninth word, vangh<i>e</i>u<i>s</i>, in the Ahunavair, according to B. P. Riv.; but it is the tenth Nask in other Rivâyats. Ka<i>s</i>kîsrôbô may perhaps mean ‘with happy, or comfortable, statements,’ and is corrupted into Ka<i>s</i>srôb, Ka<i>s</i>kasîrah, or Ka<i>s</i>kanîz, in the Rivâyats, which also state that it contained originally sixty kardah, or subdivisions, of which only fifteen were recovered after the time of Alexander.

[^2]: (23:2) The minor ceremonies.
