## EXTANT FRAGMENTS. XIX. VENDÎDÂD NASK [^1].

The whole of this Nask (as described in Dk. VIII, Chap. XLIV) is still extant, and is considered by the writers of the Persian Rivâyats to be a complete work. Its fragmentary character, which is obvious enough to European scholars, must, therefore, be <span id="p482"><sup><small>[ p. 482 ]</small></sup></span> attributed, in all probability, to losses it sustained before the revival of Ma<i>z</i><i>d</i>a-worship by the Sasanian dynasty. It is remarkable that the compiler of the account in the Dinka<i>r</i><i>d</i> makes no allusion to the twelfth fargar<i>d</i> of the Vendîdâ<i>d</i>, which is also omitted in all old MSS. of the Vendîdâ<i>d</i> with Pahlavi that have been examined, although the copyists appear to have been aware of the existence of a twelfth fargar<i>d</i>.
