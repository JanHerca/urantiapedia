At that period, at that age the Venerable Ascetic Mahâvîra had nine Ga<i>n</i>as and eleven Ga<i>n</i>adharas.

‘Why, now, has it been said, that the Venerable Ascetic Mahâvîra had nine Ga<i>n</i>as, but eleven Ga<i>n</i>adharas?’

‘The oldest monk of the Venerable Ascetic Mahâvîra was Indrabhûti of the Gautama gotra, who instructed five hundred <i>S</i>rama<i>n</i>as; the middle-aged monk was Agnibhûti of the Gautama gotra, who instructed five hundred Srama<i>n</i>as; the youngest was Vâyubhûti of the Gautama gotra, who instructed five hundred <i>S</i>rama<i>n</i>as. The Sthavira Ârya-Vyakta of the Bhâradvâ<i>g</i>a gotra instructed five hundred <i>S</i>rama<i>n</i>as; the Sthavira Ârya-Sudharman of the Agnive<i>s</i>yâyana gotra instructed five hundred <i>S</i>rama<i>n</i>as; the Sthavira Ma<i>n</i><i>d</i>ikaputra [^663] of the Vâsish<i>th</i>a gotra instructed two hundred and fifty <i>S</i>rama<i>n</i>as; the Sthavira Mauryaputra of the Kâ<i>s</i>yapa gotra instructed two hundred and fifty <i>S</i>rama<i>n</i>as; the Sthavira Akampita of the Gautama gotra and Sthavira A<i>k</i>alabhrât<i>ri</i> of the Hâritâyana gotra, both Sthaviras instructed together three hundred <i>S</i>rama<i>n</i>as each; the Sthaviras Metârya and Prabhâsa, both of the Kau<i>n</i><i>d</i>i<i>n</i>ya gotra, instructed together <span id="p287"><sup><small>[ p. 287 ]</small></sup></span> three hundred <i>S</i>rama<i>n</i>as each [^664]. Therefore, Sir, has it been said that the Venerable Ascetic Mahâvîra had nine Ga<i>n</i>as, but eleven Ga<i>n</i>adharas: (1)

All these eleven Ga<i>n</i>adharas of the Venerable Ascetic Mahâvîra, who knew the twelve Aṅgas, the fourteen Pûrvas, and the whole Siddhânta of the Ga<i>n</i>ins, died, &c. (all down to) freed from all pains in Râ<i>g</i>ag<i>ri</i>ha after fasting a month without drinking water. The Sthaviras Indrabhûti and Ârya Sudharman both died after the Nirvâ<i>n</i>a of Mahâvira. The Nirgrantha <i>S</i>rama<i>n</i>as of the present time are all (spiritual) descendants of the monk Ârya Sudharman, the rest of the Ga<i>n</i>adharas left no descendants. (2)

The Venerable Ascetic Mahâvîra was of the Kâ<i>s</i>yapa gotra. His disciple was [^665]:

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;1. Ârya Sudharman of the Agnive<i>s</i>yâyana gotra;

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;2. Ârya <i>G</i>ambûnâman of the Kâ<i>s</i>yapa gotra;

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;3. Ârya Prabhava of the Kâtyâyana gotra;

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;4. Ârya <i>S</i>ayyambha, father of Manaka, was of the Vatsa gotra;

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;5. Ârya Ya<i>s</i>obhadra of the Tuṅgikâyana gotra. (3)

In the short redaction the list of Sthaviras after .Ârya Ya<i>s</i>obhadra is the following:

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;6. Ârya Sambhûtavi<i>g</i>aya of the Mâ<i>th</i>ara gotra and Ârya Bhadrabâhu of the Prâ<i>k</i>îna gotra;

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;7. Ârya Sthûlabhadra of the Gautama gotra;

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;8. i. Ârya Mahâgiri of the Ailâpatya gotra and

<span id="p288"><sup><small>[ p. 288 ]</small></sup></span>

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;ii. Ârya Suhastin of the Vâsish<i>th</i>a gotra;

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;9. Susthita and Supratibuddha, surnamed Ko<i>t</i>ika and Kâkandaka, of the Vyâghrâpatya gotra;

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;10. Ârya Indradatta (Indadinna) of the Kau<i>s</i>ika gotra;

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;11. Ârya Datta (Dinna) of the Gautama gotra;

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;12. Ârya Si<i>m</i>hagiri <i>G</i>âtismara of the Kau<i>s</i>ika gotra;

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;13. Ârya Va<i>g</i>ra of the Gautama gotra;

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;14. Ârya Va<i>g</i>rasena of the Utk<i>ri</i>sh<i>t</i>a gotra [^666].

He had four disciples: Ârya Nâgila, Ârya Padmila, Ârya <i>G</i>ayanta, and Ârya Tâpasa, each of whom founded a <i>S</i>âkhâ called after his name, viz. the Aryanâgilâ <i>S</i>âkhâ, the Âryapadmilâ <i>S</i>âkhâ, the Ârya<i>g</i>ayantî <i>S</i>âkhâ, and the Âryatâpasî <i>S</i>âkhâ. (4)

In the detailed redaction the list of Sthaviras after Ârya Ya<i>s</i>obhadra is the following:

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;6. i. Ârya Bhadrabâhu of the Prâ<i>k</i>îna gotra, who had four disciples of the Kâ<i>s</i>yapa gotra:

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;a. Godâsa, founder of the Godâsa Ga<i>n</i>a [^667], which was divided into four <i>S</i>âkhâs:

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;α. The Tâmraliptikâ <i>S</i>âkhâ,

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;β. The Ko<i>t</i>ivarshîyâ <i>S</i>âkhâ,

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;γ. The Pu<i>n</i><i>d</i>ravardhanîyâ <i>S</i>âkhâ, and

<span id="p289"><sup><small>[ p. 289 ]</small></sup></span>

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;δ. The Dâsîkharba<i>t</i>ikâ <i>S</i>âkhâ.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;b. Agnidatta,

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;c. <i>G</i>anadatta,

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;d. Somadatta.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;ii. Ârya Sambhutavi<i>g</i>aya of the Mâ<i>th</i>ara gotra, who had twelve disciples:

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;7.  a. Nandanabhadra,

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;b. Upananda,

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;c. Tishyabhadra [^668],

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;d. Ya<i>s</i>obhadra,

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;e. Sumanobhadra [^669],

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;f. Ma<i>n</i>ibhadra,

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;g. Pu<i>n</i>yabhadra [^670],

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;h. Sthûlabhadra of the Gautama gotra,

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;i. Ri<i>g</i>umati,

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;k. <i>G</i>ambû,

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;l. Dîrghabhadra, and

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;m. Pâ<i>n</i><i>d</i>ubhadra;

and seven female disciples:

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;a. Yakshâ,

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;b. Yakshadattâ (Yakshadinnâ),

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;c. Bhûtâ,

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;d. Bhûtadattâ (Bhûtadinnâ),

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;e. Senâ (also E<i>n</i>â),

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;f. Ve<i>n</i>â,

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;g Re<i>n</i>â.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;8. i. Ârya Mahâgiri of the Ailâpatya gotra, who had eight disciples:

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;a. Uttara,

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;b. Balissaha, who both together founded the Uttarabalissaha Ga<i>n</i>a, which was divided into four <i>S</i>âkhâs

<span id="p290"><sup><small>[ p. 290 ]</small></sup></span>

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;α. Kau<i>s</i>ambikâ,

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;β. Sautaptikâ (Pr. Soittiyâ),

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;γ. Kau<i>t</i>umbinî (or Ku<i>n</i><i>d</i>adharî),

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;δ. <i>K</i>andanâgarî.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;c. Dhanarddhi (Pr. Dha<i>n</i>a<i>d</i><i>dh</i>a),

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;d. <i>S</i>irarddhi (Pr. Siri<i>d</i><i>dh</i>a),

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;e. Ko<i>d</i>i<i>n</i>ya,

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;f. Nâga,

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;g. Nâgaputra,

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;h. <i>Kh</i>aluka Rohagupta of the Kau<i>s</i>ika gotra, founder of the Trairâ<i>s</i>ika <i>S</i>âkhâ.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;ii. Ârya Suhastin [^671] of the Vâsish<i>th</i>a gotra, who had twelve disciples:

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;9.  a. Ârya Roha<i>n</i>a of the Kâ<i>s</i>yapa gotra, founder of the Uddeha Ga<i>n</i>a, which was divided into four <i>S</i>âkhâs:

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;α. Udumbarikâ (Pr. Udumbari<i>g</i><i>g</i>iyâ),

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;β. Mâsapûrikâ,

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;γ. Matipatrikâ,

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;δ. Pûr<i>n</i>apatrikâ (Pr. Punnapattiyâ, Panna°, Sunna°, or Suvanna°);

and into six Kulas:

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;α´. Nâgabhûta,

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;β´. Somabhûta,

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;γ´. Ullaga<i>k</i><i>kh</i>a (or Ârdraka<i>k</i><i>kh</i>a?),

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;δ´. Hastilipta (Pr. Hatthili<i>g</i><i>g</i>a),

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;ε´. Nândika (Pr. Nandi<i>g</i><i>g</i>a),

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;ζ´. Parihâsaka.

<span id="p291"><sup><small>[ p. 291 ]</small></sup></span>

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;b. Bhadraya<i>s</i>as of the Bhâradvâ<i>g</i>a gotra, who founded the U<i>d</i>uvâ<i>t</i>ika Ga<i>n</i>a, which was divided into four <i>S</i>âkhâs:

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;α. Kampîyikâ (Pr. Ka<i>m</i>pi<i>g</i><i>g</i>iyâ),

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;β. Bhadrîyikâ (Pr. Bhaddi<i>g</i><i>g</i>iyâ),

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;γ. Kâkandikâ,

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;δ. Mekhalîyikâ (Pr. Mehali<i>g</i><i>g</i>iyâ);

and into three Kulas:

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;α´. Bhadrayaska (Pr. Bhadda<i>g</i>asiya),

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;β´. Bhadraguptika,

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;γ´. Ya<i>s</i>obhadra (Pr. <i>G</i>asabhadda).

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;c. Megha.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;d. Kâmarddhi (Pr. Kâmi<i>d</i><i>dh</i>i) of the Ku<i>n</i><i>d</i>ala gotra, who founded the Ve<i>s</i>avâ<i>t</i>ika Ga<i>n</i>a, which was divided into four <i>S</i>âkhâs:

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;α. <i>S</i>râvastikâ,

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;β. Râ<i>g</i>yapâlikâ (Pr. Ra<i>g</i><i>g</i>apâliyâ),

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;γ. Antara<i>ñ</i><i>g</i>ikâ (Pr. Antari<i>g</i><i>g</i>iyâ),

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;δ. Kshemaliptikâ (Pr. Khemali<i>g</i><i>g</i>iyâ);

and into four Kulas:

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;α´. Ga<i>n</i>ika,

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;β´. Maighika,

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;γ´. Kâmarddhika,

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;δ´. Indrapuraka.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;e. <i>S</i>rîgupta of the Hârita gotra, founder of the <i>K</i>âra<i>n</i>a Ga<i>n</i>a, which was divided into four <i>S</i>âkhâs:

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;α. Hâritamâlâkârî,

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;β. Sa<i>m</i>kâ<i>s</i>ikâ,

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;γ. Gavedhukâ,

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;δ. Va<i>g</i>ranâgarî;

and into seven Kulas:

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;α´. Vâtsalîya (Pr. Va<i>k</i><i>kh</i>ali<i>g</i><i>g</i>a),

<span id="p292"><sup><small>[ p. 292 ]</small></sup></span>

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;β´. Prîtidharmika,

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;γ´. Hâridraka (Pr. Hâli<i>g</i><i>g</i>a),

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;δ´. Pushyamitrika (Pr. Pûsamitti<i>g</i><i>g</i>a),

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;ε´. Mâlyaka (Pr. Mâli<i>g</i><i>g</i>a),

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;ζ´. Ârya<i>k</i>e<i>t</i>aka,

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;η´. K<i>ri</i>sh<i>n</i>asakha (Pr. Kanhasaha).

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;f. <i>Ri</i>shigupta Kâkandaka of the Vâsish<i>th</i>a gotra, founder of the Mânava Ga<i>n</i>a, which was divided into four <i>S</i>âkhâs:

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;α. Kâ<i>s</i>yapîyâ (Pr. Kâsavi<i>g</i><i>g</i>iyâ),

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;β. Gautamîyâ (Pr. Goyame<i>g</i><i>g</i>iyâ),

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;γ. Vâsish<i>th</i>îyâ (Pr. Vâsi<i>t</i><i>th</i>iyâ),

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;δ. Saurâsh<i>t</i>rikâ;

and into three Kulas:

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;α´. <i>Ri</i>shiguptika,

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;β´. <i>Ri</i>shidattika,

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;γ´. Abhiya<i>s</i>asa.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;g. and h. Susthita and Supratibuddha, surnamed Kau<i>t</i>ika and Kâkandaka, of the Vyâghrâpatya gotra, founders of the Kau<i>t</i>ika Ga<i>n</i>a, which was divided into four <i>S</i>âkhâs:

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;α. U<i>k</i><i>k</i>anâgarî,

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;β. Vidyâdharî,

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;γ. Va<i>g</i>rî,

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;δ. Madhyamikâ (Pr. Ma<i>g</i><i>gh</i>imilla);

and into four Kulas:

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;α´. Brahmaliptaka (Pr. Ba<i>m</i>bhalig<i>g</i>a),

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;β´. Vâtsalîya (Pr. Va<i>k</i><i>kh</i>ali<i>g</i><i>g</i>a, cf. e. α´.),

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;γ´. Vâ<i>n</i>îya (Pr. Vâ<i>n</i>i<i>g</i><i>g</i>a),

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;δ´. Pra<i>s</i>navâhanaka.

Both Sthaviras had together five disciples:

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;10. a. Ârya Indradatta (Pr. Indadinna) of the Kâ<i>s</i>yapa gotra,

<span id="p293"><sup><small>[ p. 293 ]</small></sup></span>

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;b. Priyagantha, founder of the Madhyamâ <i>S</i>âkhâ,

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;c. Vidyâdharagopâla of the Kâ<i>s</i>yapa gotra, founder of the Vidyâdharî <i>S</i>âkhâ,

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;d. <i>Ri</i>shidatta,

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;e. Arhaddatta (Pr. Arihadatta).

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;11. Ârya Datta (Pr. Dinna) of the Gautama gotra, who had two disciples:

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;12. i. Ârya <i>S</i>ântisenika of the Mâ<i>th</i>ara gotra, founder of the U<i>k</i><i>k</i>anâgarî <i>S</i>âkhâ, who had four disciples:

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;a. Ârya Senika, founder of the Âryasenikâ <i>S</i>âkhâ,

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;b. Ârya Tâpasa, founder of the Âryatâpasî <i>S</i>âkhâ,

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;c. Ârya Kubera, founder of the Âryakuberâ <i>S</i>âkhâ, and

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;d. Ârya <i>Ri</i>shipâlita, founder of the Ârya<i>ri</i>shipâlitâ <i>S</i>âkhâ.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;ii. Ârya Si<i>m</i>hagiri <i>G</i>âtismara of the Gautama gotra, who had four disciples:

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;13. a. Dhanagiri,

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;b. Ârya Samita of the Gautama gotra, founder of the Brahmadvîpikâ <i>S</i>âkhâ,

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;c. Ârya Va<i>g</i>ra of the Gautama gotra, founder of the Âryava<i>g</i>râ <i>S</i>âkhâ,

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;d. Arhaddatta (Pr. Arihadinna).

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;14. i. Ârya Va<i>g</i>rasena, founder of the Aryanâgilâ <i>S</i>âkhâ,

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;ii. Ârya Padma, founder of the Âryapadmâ <i>S</i>âkhâ,

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;iii. Ârya Ratha of the Vatsa gotra, founder of the Ârya<i>g</i>ayantî <i>S</i>âkhâ.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;15. Ârya Pushyagiri of the Kau<i>s</i>ika gotra.

<span id="p294"><sup><small>[ p. 294 ]</small></sup></span>

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;16. Ârya Phalgumitra of the Gautama gotra.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;17. Ârya Dhanagiri of the Vâsish<i>th</i>a gotra.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;18. Ârya <i>S</i>ivabhûti of the Kautsa gotra.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;19. Ârya Bhadra of the Kâ<i>s</i>yapa gotra.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;20. Ârya Nakshatra of the Kâ<i>s</i>yapa gotra.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;21. Ârya Raksha of the Kâ<i>s</i>yapa gotra.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;22. Ârya Nâga of the Gautama gotra.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;23. Ârya <i>G</i>ehila [^672] of the Vâsish<i>th</i>a gotra.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;24. Ârya Vish<i>n</i>u of the Mât<i>h</i>ara gotra.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;25. Ârya Kâlaka of the Gautama gotra.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;26. Ârya Sampalita and Bhadra, both of the Gautama gotra.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;27. Ârya V<i>ri</i>ddha of the Gautama gotra.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;28. Ârya Saṅghapâlita of the Gautama gotra.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;29. Ârya Hastin of the Kâ<i>s</i>yapa gotra.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;30. Ârya Dharma of the Suvrata gotra.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;31. Ârya Si<i>m</i>ha of the Kâ<i>s</i>yapa gotra.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;32. Ârya Dharma of the Kâ<i>s</i>yapa gotra.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;33. Ârya <i>S</i>â<i>n</i><i>d</i>ilya [^673].

<span id="p295"><sup><small>[ p. 295 ]</small></sup></span>

Bowing down my head, I pay my reverence to the Sthavira <i>G</i>ambû of the Gautama gotra, who possessed steady virtue, good conduct, and knowledge. ix.

I prostrate myself before the Sthavira Nandita of Kâ<i>s</i>yapa gotra, who is possessed of great clemency and of knowledge, intuition, and good conduct. x.

Then I adore the Kshamâ<i>s</i>rama<i>n</i>a De<i>s</i>iga<i>n</i>in of the Kâ<i>s</i>yapa gotra, who, steady in his conduct, possesses the highest righteousness and virtue. xi.

Then I prostrate myself before the Kshamâ<i>s</i>rama<i>n</i>a Sthiragupta of the Vâtsya gotra, the preserver of the sacred lore, the wise one, the ocean of wisdom, him of great virtue. xii.

Then I adore the Sthavira prince, Dharma, the virtuous Ga<i>n</i>in, who stands well in knowledge, intuition, good conduct, and penance, and is rich in virtues [^674]. xiii.

I revere the Kshamâ<i>s</i>rama<i>n</i>a Devarddhi of the Kâ<i>s</i>yapa gotra, who wears, as it were, the jewel of the right understanding of the Sûtras, and possesses the virtues of patience, self-restraint, and clemency. xiv.

End of the List of the Sthaviras.
