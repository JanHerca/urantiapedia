P. [ix](Preface#pix), _note_: For “edition” read “translation.”

[14](2#p14), line 3: For “by” read “in the.”

„ [16](2#p16), line 5: For “T.” read “_T‘u Shu_.”

„ „ § 19, _note:_ Before “War” insert “Soldiers are not to be used as playthings.”

„ [17](3#p17), § 1: #, etc. The more I think about it, the more I prefer the rendering suggested on p. [159](12#p159), § 22, _note_.

„ „ § 1 _note_, and p. [78](8#p78), line 6: Insert “the” before “Ssŭ-ma Fa.”

„ [33](5#p33), note on heading: Cf. X. § 12, where # is translated “strength,” though it might also be “conditions.” The three words #, # and # have been much confused. It appears from the _Shuo Wên_ that the last character is post-classical, so that Sun Tzŭ must have used either or a in all senses.

„ [45](6#p45), line 1: For “sublety” read “subtlety.”

„ [63](7#p63), line 4: M. Chavannes writes in the _T‘oung Pao_, 1906, p. 210: “Le général Pan Tch‘ao n’a jamais porté les armes chinoises jusque sur les bords de la mer Caspienne.” I hasten to correct my statement on this authority.

„ [80](9#p80), 9th line from the bottom: For # read #.

„ [100](10#p100), § 23, _note_, and p. [126](11#p126), 5th line from bottom: For “Huang Shih-kung” read “Huang-shih Kung.”

„ [124](11#p124), line 7: For “Ch‘ên” read “Ch‘ên Hao.”

„ [136](11#p136), 11th line from bottom: Insert “to” before “select.”

„ [152](12#p152), § 2: Substitute semi-colon for full stop after “available.”
